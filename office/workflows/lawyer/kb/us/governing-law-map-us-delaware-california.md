# Governing-law map: Delaware vs California vs federal law

**Last updated:** 2026-04-17

---

## 1. Why this map matters

The Horizon agreement combines at least three legal layers:

| Layer | Why it matters here |
|---|---|
| **Delaware corporate law** | Horizon is a Delaware corporation; board authority, stock grants, director-conflict safe harbors, and indemnification are Delaware questions |
| **California contract law / statutory policy** | The agreement says California law governs; restrictive covenants and some contract enforceability questions are therefore heavily California-shaped |
| **Federal law** | Securities, tax, IP, trade secrets, and parts of worker classification sit at federal level |

The mistake to avoid is assuming that “Delaware company” means Delaware law governs everything. It does not.

## 2. Issue-by-issue map

| Issue | Main law likely to matter most | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Validity of stock option / restricted stock authorization | **Delaware** + company charter/plan docs | Issuance authority, board approval, plan authority, authorized shares |
| Exercise price / FMV / 409A risk | **Federal tax** + board process | Tax law sets the 409A framework; board process and valuation support matter |
| Rule 701 availability | **Federal securities** | Private-company compensatory grant exemption |
| Non-compete / post-termination restraint | **California** | Agreement selects California law; California strongly disfavors restraints |
| Broad outside-work disclosure duty | **California contract / public policy** | Enforceability and overbreadth analysis |
| Independent contractor vs employee risk | **California + federal** | California ABC test, IRS control test, FLSA economic-reality test |
| Interested-director / conflict cleansing | **Delaware** | DGCL § 144 and fiduciary duty framework |
| Corporate opportunity issues | **Delaware** | Duty of loyalty / corporate opportunity doctrine, with possible charter renunciation |
| Confidentiality / trade-secret notice | **Federal + state** | DTSA immunity notice plus state trade-secret / contract law |
| IP assignment / work made for hire | **Federal IP law** + contract | Copyright and patent rules are federal |
| Indemnification / exculpation | **Delaware** | DGCL §§ 102(b)(7), 145, charter/bylaws/indemnification agreement |
| Tax withholding / self-employment taxes | **Federal** + state tax law | Depends on classification and grant type |

## 3. What Delaware likely controls here

### Board and stock authority
A Delaware corporation is managed under board authority, and stock issuances / option grants must fit the Delaware General Corporation Law, the certificate of incorporation, bylaws, and any equity plan.

### Interested-director transactions
If the advisor also has a role at another company that contracts with Horizon, Delaware law matters for how Horizon’s board approves or cleanses conflict situations.

### Indemnification and exculpation
Whether the company can protect directors/officers (and sometimes others by contract) is usually driven by DGCL rules plus the charter/bylaws.

---

## 4. What California likely controls here

### Restrictive covenants
California Business and Professions Code § 16600 strongly disfavors contractual restraints on lawful trade or profession, especially in the employment context.

### Outside-work disclosure / prior-notice clause
Even if framed as “conflict management,” a clause that effectively gives a company veto power over non-competing advisory work can face overbreadth scrutiny.

### Employee-specific forum / choice-of-law protections
If a worker primarily resides and works in California and is really an employee, Labor Code § 925 can override some out-of-state forum/choice-of-law provisions unless the worker was individually represented by counsel in negotiating the term.

---

## 5. What federal law likely controls here

### Securities
Rule 701 can exempt private-company compensatory grants to employees, directors, officers, consultants, and advisors, subject to conditions.

### Tax
- **IRC § 409A** matters for discounted options and valuation support.
- **IRC § 83 / 83(b)** matters for restricted stock and some early-exercise cases.
- **ISO vs NSO** rules matter because advisors are usually non-employees.

### IP and trade secrets
- Copyright ownership rules and “work made for hire” limits are federal.
- Patent assignments require writing.
- DTSA immunity notice can matter in confidentiality agreements.

---

## 6. Practical takeaway for this agreement

A good review should separate four buckets:

1. **Delaware corporate validity**  
   Is the promised equity actually grantable, properly approved, and correctly described?

2. **California enforceability of restrictions**  
   Is the non-compete / outside-work / conflict package too broad?

3. **Federal tax/securities mechanics**  
   Is the equity grant tax-safe, securities-compliant, and documented?

4. **Federal/state classification + IP**  
   Is the advisor really treated as a contractor, and does the IP/confidentiality language do more than it should?
