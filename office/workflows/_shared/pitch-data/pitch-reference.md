# Pitch Deck Reference

Synthesized from YC, Sequoia, a16z, Kawasaki, and First Round Capital.

---

## Investor Pitch — Consensus Structure (12-15 slides)

| # | Slide | Purpose | What It Must Contain |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | Title / Company Purpose | Identity | Company name + one-line description |
| 2 | Problem (Data) | Pain | Who hurts, how much, current workarounds. Use one strong data point |
| 3 | Problem (Human) | Empathy | Real situations, lived experience, current pain |
| 4 | Solution | Relief | What you do in fewest words possible. Concrete benefits, not features |
| 5 | Before / After | Contrast | Visual transformation from old world to new world |
| 6 | Why Now | Timing | Tech, regulatory, or behavioral tailwind making this the right moment |
| 7 | Traction | Proof | Revenue, users, MoM growth — make numbers meaningful with context |
| 8 | Market Size | Scale | TAM/SAM/SOM with bottom-up methodology |
| 9 | Competition | Position | 2-axis quadrant (you top-right) or clear differentiation table |
| 10 | Business Model | Economics | Who pays, how much, gross margin, unit economics |
| 11 | Team | Trust | Founders only. One photo, one past win each. What makes them uniquely suited |
| 12 | The Ask | Action | How much you need, what it buys, where you'll be in 12 months |
| 13 | Vision (optional) | Dream | 10-word world domination line |

**Rule:** If any section needs expansion, add slides — keep any set n <= 3. Total: 12-15 max plus appendix.

## Client Pitch — Recommended Structure (10-12 slides)

| # | Slide | Purpose | What It Must Contain |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | Title | Identity | Company name + client-facing tagline |
| 2 | Their Problem | Empathy | Specific pain the client experiences, with data |
| 3 | Current Reality | Gap | How they solve it today and why it fails |
| 4 | Solution | Value | What you do for them, described in their language |
| 5 | How It Works | Clarity | Step-by-step or workflow visual |
| 6 | Before / After | Proof | Concrete transformation with metrics |
| 7 | Case Studies / Proof | Trust | Client results, testimonials, logos |
| 8 | Why Us | Differentiation | What makes you the right choice vs alternatives |
| 9 | Pricing | Decision | Clear plans, ROI framing vs cost of status quo |
| 10 | Next Steps | CTA | Timeline, contact, what happens next |

## What Investors Screen For

Investors scan decks in 10-60 seconds asking five questions:

1. Do I trust the team?
2. Is there early proof?
3. Is this venture-scale?
4. Does it match my thesis?
5. Do I understand it quickly?

If any answer is "no," the deck gets rejected.

**Average deck review time:** 2:40 - 4:10. Only ~22% receive a complete read-through. Drop-off zone: pages 5-9.

**Implication:** Front-load the strongest slides. The deck must work standalone without narration.

## Three Non-Negotiable Slide Qualities (Kevin Hale, YC)

1. **Legible** — High contrast, simple bold fonts, large type. Key text at top.
2. **Simple** — Each slide expresses ONE idea.
3. **Obvious** — Understood at a glance. If a stranger can't immediately say your idea, the slide fails.

## Design Standards

### Typography

- 1-2 fonts maximum (one body, one headings)
- Clean sans-serif for body
- 24pt+ body minimum, 30pt per Kawasaki
- Bold for key points, never long paragraphs

### Layout

- One focal element per slide with descriptive title stating the point
- 15-20% minimum whitespace
- One message per slide
- Design for skim-reading

### Color

- Align with brand identity
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- Consistent application across all slides

### Data Visualization

- Label graphs explicitly — write the takeaway ("Revenue up 340% YoY")
- Simplify charts ruthlessly
- Consistent styles across deck

### Slide Density

- 5-7 lines per slide, 5-7 words per line
- Design for skim-reading with subheadings and visual hierarchy

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better Approach |
|---------|----------------|
| Starting with solution instead of problem | Lead with painful problem + data |
| Vague market sizing ("$50B market") | Bottom-up calculation with methodology |
| No traction at all | Show any proof: early customers, revenue, pilots, LOIs |
| Unclear ask | State exact amount + what it gets you + 12-month milestones |
| Too many slides (30+) | 12-15 main + appendix |
| Screenshots | Almost always illegible — use clean illustrations |
| Paragraphs of text | Slides are visual aids, not scripts |
| Default templates | Custom design shows effort and taste |
| Complex diagrams | "Little mazes for ideas" — simplify |

## Seed vs. Series A

| Dimension | Seed | Series A |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Narrative | Vision + problem insight + team | Execution + repeatable growth |
| Traction | Pilots, early revenue, LOIs | MRR, CAC, LTV, churn, velocity |
| Product | MVP, mockups, roadmap | Launched with PMF evidence |
| Business Model | "We'll charge subscription" | Proven conversion + retention |
| Risk | Platform risk (right thing?) | Execution risk (can you scale?) |
