# Meeting Type: Discovery / Exploration

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What are you trying to discover or learn from this meeting? | Core research objective |
| Who will you be speaking with? Background? | How to frame questions, expected depth of answers |
| What's the relationship — prospect, user, expert, potential hire? | Formality level, what you can ask |
| How did this meeting come about? | Their expectations and willingness to share |

### Relationship History
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Any prior interactions with this person or their organization? | Context for the conversation |
| What do they think this meeting is about? | Expectation alignment |
| What's in it for them? Why did they agree to meet? | Their motivation — affects candor |

### Hypotheses
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What do you believe is true that you want to validate? | Hypotheses to test |
| What do you suspect but aren't sure about? | Open questions to explore |
| What would change your mind or surprise you? | High-value signals to watch for |

### Objectives
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What are the 3 most important things to learn? | Prioritization for limited time |
| What decisions depend on what you learn here? | Stakes — what this meeting unlocks |
| What impression do you want to leave? | Whether to be neutral researcher or engaged partner |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Any topics to avoid or approach carefully? | Sensitivity calibration |
| Do you want them to talk mostly, or is it a dialogue? | Meeting structure |
| How much should you reveal about your own work? | Information asymmetry management |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format? | Pacing and question depth |

## Output Skeleton

### {Title} — Discovery Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Contexto
Table: discovery objective, contact, their organization, relationship, format, duration.

#### Postura geral
Overall approach — neutral researcher, curious peer, or engaged partner. How much to reveal. How to keep them talking.

#### Relationship Context
What's known about this person or organization. Prior interactions, their motivation for the meeting, what they expect.

#### Hypotheses to Validate
Numbered list of beliefs or assumptions to test. Each with: the hypothesis, how to test it (specific question or probe), what a confirming vs. disconfirming answer looks like.

#### Question Flow
Ordered sequence of questions organized by theme. Not a rigid script — a flow that builds naturally from broad to specific. Each question with:
- The question itself
- What it reveals
- Follow-up probes if the answer is interesting
- Transition to next theme

Instruction: "Follow the flow but follow the energy. If they open up on a topic, go deep. Skip questions already answered."

#### Signals to Watch For
Table: signal (something they say or do) → what it means → how to respond. Specific behavioral or verbal cues that indicate important information.

#### Things to Say
Table: situation → what to say. Opening lines, transition phrases, probing language, closing.

#### Things NOT to Say
Table: statement to avoid → why. Language that would shut down disclosure, bias their answers, or create wrong expectations.

#### Closing & Next Steps
How to close the conversation gracefully. How to ask for follow-up access. How to leave the door open for future conversations.
