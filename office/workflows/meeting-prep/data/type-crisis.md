# Meeting Type: Crisis / Damage Control

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What happened? What's the situation? | Core facts — separate what happened from interpretation |
| When did it happen and when did the other party find out? | Timeline and whether delay in communication is part of the problem |
| Who is affected? Who are you meeting with? | Stakeholder map and who's most upset |
| What's the current state — is the issue resolved, ongoing, or escalating? | Urgency level |

### Relationship History
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What was the relationship like before this incident? | Trust reservoir — how much goodwill exists to draw on |
| Any prior incidents or tensions? | Pattern risk — this might feel like "the last straw" |
| Who else knows about the situation? | Containment status |

### Accountability
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What was your role in what happened? What's genuinely your fault? | What to own vs. what to contextualize |
| Were there factors outside your control? | Honest framing without excuse-making |
| What have you already done to address it? | Actions speak louder — show remediation |

### Objectives
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What outcome do you need — forgiveness, continued relationship, damage limitation? | Realistic goal setting |
| What's the worst-case scenario if this meeting goes badly? | Stakes and fallback planning |
| What are you willing to offer — compensation, concessions, commitments? | Recovery toolkit |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Do they want an explanation, an apology, a plan, or all three? | Meeting structure |
| What's their emotional state — angry, disappointed, pragmatic? | Tone calibration |
| What would feel hollow or insincere to them? | Anti-patterns for this specific audience |
| What impression do you want to leave? | Even in crisis — what's the residual perception |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format? | In-person is almost always better for crisis |
| Who's on your side? | Coordination — who apologizes, who presents the plan |

## Output Skeleton

### {Title} — Crisis Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Contexto
Table: incident summary, affected party, participants, current state (resolved/ongoing/escalating), format, duration.

#### Postura geral
Overall approach — lead with accountability, not defense. Tone guidance: serious, honest, non-performative. What to project: that you understand the gravity and are taking action.

#### Relationship Context
Relationship before the incident. Trust level, prior interactions, any history of tensions. What goodwill exists to draw on.

#### Situation Timeline
Chronological facts: what happened, when, what was done, when the other party was informed. Factual, no spin. This is your reference for staying honest during the meeting.

#### Accountability Framing
What to own explicitly — in direct language, no hedging. What's context (not excuse) — external factors stated honestly without deflecting responsibility. Specific language for taking accountability.

#### What You've Already Done
Numbered list of actions taken since the incident. Concrete, verifiable. Shows you didn't wait for this meeting to act.

#### Recovery Plan
What you're committing to going forward. Each commitment with: what, when, how they'll know it's done. Only commit to what you can deliver — broken recovery promises are worse than the original incident.

#### What to Say
Table: moment → what to say. Opening acknowledgment, accountability statement, plan presentation, response to anger, closing commitment.

#### What NOT to Say
Table: statement to avoid → why. Defensive language, minimizing phrases, blame-shifting, premature optimism. Anything that would feel hollow.

#### If It Escalates
What to do if the meeting turns hostile. De-escalation language. When to pause. When to offer to reconvene. Never match their energy — absorb it.

#### Worst-Case Protocol
If the relationship is ending: how to close gracefully, what to offer, how to leave the door open for the future.
