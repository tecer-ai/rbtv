# Meeting Type: Sales / Demo

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What does this company do? What's their scale? | Industry, complexity, whether they're a fit |
| Who will be in the room? Names, titles, roles in the decision? | Power dynamics, who to convince, who to neutralize |
| How did this meeting come about? Cold outreach, referral, inbound? | Relationship temperature, how much they already know |
| What do they already know about you/your product? | How much context-setting is needed vs. jumping to value |

### Relationship
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Any prior interactions — emails, calls, mutual connections? | Warmth level, existing trust or skepticism |
| Do you know anything about their current systems or tools? | Integration angle, competitive displacement risk |
| Any personal connections to people in the room? | Trust anchors to leverage naturally |

### Objectives
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What's the single most important outcome for this meeting? | Prioritization — demo vs. discovery vs. closing |
| What would make this meeting a failure? | Red lines, traps to avoid |
| Are you trying to close something or open a relationship? | Aggressiveness calibration |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What pain points do you think they have that you solve? | Hypothesis for discovery questions |
| What objections do you expect? | Defensive preparation |
| What should you absolutely NOT say or promise? | Calibration guardrails |
| What impression do you want to leave? | Tone and posture guidance |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format (remote/in-person)? | Pacing and phase timing |
| Who's on your side? Roles during the meeting? | Coordination, who speaks when |

## Output Skeleton

### {Title} — {Meeting Type} Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Contexto
Table: contact type, temperature, prior context, participants (both sides), format, duration.

#### Postura geral
2-3 paragraphs on overall approach, tone, and behavioral guidance. What impression to give. What to prioritize (e.g., "listen first, present second").

#### The Room
Table per participant on the other side: name, title, what they care about, your approach with them.

#### Intelligence Nuggets
Numbered list of 3-6 strategic insights about the other party — connections, background, timing, industry context. Each with a tactical implication.

#### Meeting Phases
Table with timed blocks: phase name, minutes, what happens, specific guidance. Total should match meeting duration.

#### Discovery Questions
Prioritized list of questions to ask during the meeting, each with "what this reveals" annotation. Instruction: "Ask in this order. The first one that gets traction — go deep."

#### Key Anchoring Points
Table: situation/trigger → what to say. Specific language for predictable moments.

#### Things NOT to Say
Table: statement to avoid → why it's dangerous. Direct, blunt reasons.

#### Next Steps (graduated)
Table by interest level (high/medium/low): what to propose as next step for each scenario.

#### Calibration Table
Table: topic → what to say → what NOT to say. Covers the most sensitive areas.
