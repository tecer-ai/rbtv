# Meeting Type: Investor

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Which fund/investor, and who specifically will you meet? | Tailor the pitch to their thesis and the person's background |
| What stage and check size does this fund typically do? | Whether this is a fit conversation or a relationship-building one |
| How did this meeting come about? | Temperature — warm intro vs. cold application |
| What do they already know about your company? | How much context-setting the opener needs |

### Investor Research
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Do you know their investment thesis or focus areas? | Framing alignment |
| Any portfolio companies that compete with or complement you? | Conflict detection, positioning landmines |
| Any portfolio companies in your space that failed or struggled? | What NOT to remind them of |
| What's the contact person's background — IB, operator, consulting? | Language calibration (financial vs. product vs. technical) |

### Relationship
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Have you met this fund before? Any prior calls or interactions? | Continuity — what worked/failed last time |
| Have you pitched other funds before this one? What worked, what failed? | Cross-call learnings to apply |
| Any mutual connections or warm references? | Trust anchors |

### Objectives
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Are you raising now, or building a relationship for later? | Fundraising narrative framing |
| What's your ask — if any? | Whether to close or keep open |
| What questions do you want to ask THEM? | Qualifying the investor, showing maturity |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What's your current traction — users, revenue, design partners? | Data points to prepare |
| What are the 2-3 hardest questions they could ask? | Defensive preparation |
| Any topics to avoid — co-founder dynamics, burn rate, technical depth? | Calibration guardrails |
| What impression do you want to leave? | Tone — confident, humble, scrappy, technical? |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format? | Pacing |
| Who's on your side? | Role coordination |

## Output Skeleton

### {Fund Name} Call — Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Critical Intelligence
Fund-specific angle that changes how you approach the call. Portfolio conflicts, recent investments in the space, fund dynamics. This section only appears when there's something genuinely critical.

#### Who Is {Contact Name}
Table: role, previous experience, education, connections, tenure at fund. Followed by "What this tells you" — tactical implications of their background for how to pitch.

#### The 2-Minute Opener
Four blocks of 30 seconds each: Problem, Solution, Why Us, Where We Are. Scripted language the user can internalize. Instruction: "Say this in under 2 minutes. Then stop. Let them ask questions."

#### Tailoring for {Contact's Background}
Language substitution table: "Instead of [generic] → say [tailored to their background]." Only if the contact's background warrants specific language adaptation.

#### Data Points
Table: metric, number, context. Ready to drop into answers — not to present unprompted.

#### Competition — Short Answer
Scripted 15-second answer for "who do you compete with?" Plus sub-answers for specific competitors they might name.

#### Fundraising Narrative
Recommended frame (one paragraph). Plus DO NOT list — specific things to never say about fundraising.

#### Questions to Ask {Contact/Fund}
Numbered list of 3-5 questions that qualify the investor and show maturity. Each with a subtle tactical note.

#### Traps to Avoid
Table: trap → what to do instead. Common failure modes specific to this type of meeting.

#### Cross-Call Learnings
What has worked across previous investor calls (keep doing). What has failed (stop doing). Only populated if prior cheat sheets or debriefs exist.
