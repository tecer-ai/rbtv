# Meeting Type: Hiring / Interview

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Are you the interviewer or the candidate? | Completely changes the cheat sheet structure |
| What role is this for? | Framing and evaluation criteria |
| What stage of the process — first call, technical, final, offer? | Depth and formality calibration |
| Who will be on the other side? | Tailoring to the person's role and background |

### Relationship History
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Any prior interactions — emails, calls, referrals? | Existing impressions, what they already know |
| How did this opportunity come about — applied, headhunted, referred? | Temperature and their level of interest |
| Do you know anyone at the company/who knows the candidate? | Reference points and connection hooks |

### If Interviewing (you're the interviewer)
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What are the must-have qualifications vs. nice-to-haves? | Evaluation priorities |
| What's the team culture? What personality traits matter? | Cultural fit assessment |
| What have previous people in this role struggled with? | Red flags to probe for |
| What are the deal-breakers? | Hard disqualifiers |

### If Being Interviewed (you're the candidate)
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What attracts you to this role/company? | Authenticity in your pitch |
| What's your strongest qualification for this specific role? | Lead story to prepare |
| What's your biggest weakness relative to this role? | Honest framing preparation |
| What questions do you have for them? | Showing thoughtfulness and qualifying the opportunity |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What impression do you want to leave? | Tone — confident, curious, senior, humble |
| Any sensitive topics — salary, gaps, reasons for leaving? | Framing preparation |
| What would make you say no to this? | Your own evaluation criteria |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format? | Pacing |

## Output Skeleton (adapts based on interviewer vs. candidate)

### {Title} — Interview Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Contexto
Table: role, company, stage, interviewer/candidate, other party, format, duration.

#### Postura geral
Overall approach — what to project, how formal, what energy to bring.

#### About the Other Party
What's known about the interviewer(s) or candidate. Background, role, what they likely care about.

#### If Interviewer: Evaluation Framework
Table: dimension → must-have → nice-to-have → red flag. Clear criteria for assessing the candidate.

#### If Interviewer: Question Flow
Structured questions organized by dimension. Each with: the question, what a strong answer looks like, what a weak answer looks like, follow-up probes.

#### If Candidate: Your Story
Key talking points about your background, organized by what's most relevant to this role. Not a chronological autobiography — a curated narrative.

#### If Candidate: Prepared Answers
Table: likely question → your answer (concise, structured). Cover: "tell me about yourself," "why this role," "biggest challenge," weakness, salary expectations.

#### If Candidate: Questions to Ask Them
Numbered list of thoughtful questions that show preparation and qualify the opportunity. Each with a note on what the answer reveals.

#### Things to Say
Table: situation → what to say. Opening, transitions, closing, and responses to awkward moments.

#### Things NOT to Say
Table: statement to avoid → why.

#### Evaluation Criteria (your own)
What you're evaluating about them — regardless of whether you're interviewer or candidate. After the meeting, check these against what you learned.
