# Brand Prism (Kapferer)

**Purpose:** Task-based guide for using Jean-Noel Kapferer's Brand Identity Prism to define brand identity through six interconnected facets in M3 Brand Step 3.

**Context:**
Read `brand_process.md` before starting. Execute in M3 Brand Step 3: Map Brand Identity. MUST complete Brand Archetypes (Step 2) first. The archetype selection feeds directly into the Personality facet of the prism.

---

## Framework Overview

Jean-Noel Kapferer's Brand Identity Prism is a hexagonal model that defines brand identity through six facets arranged along two axes. The vertical axis separates the **sender** (brand) from the **receiver** (customer). The horizontal axis separates **externalisation** (how the brand appears to others) from **internalisation** (how the brand and customer feel inside). The six facets are: Physique, Personality, Culture, Relationship, Reflection, and Self-Image. Together they form a coherent identity system where no single facet can be defined in isolation.

The prism matters because it prevents the common failure of defining a brand as just a logo (Physique alone) or just a personality (Personality alone). A brand that has a bold visual identity but no articulated culture will drift. A brand that defines its personality through archetypes but never considers how the customer sees themselves through it (Reflection and Self-Image) will feel self-absorbed. Kapferer's model forces completeness: you must define both sides of the brand-customer relationship, both the external signals and the internal meanings.

For startup brand work in M3, the prism serves as the central identity document that synthesises all prior inputs. The archetype from Step 2 populates the Personality facet. JTBD emotional and social jobs inform Reflection and Self-Image. Working Backwards customer language shapes Physique and Relationship. Lean Canvas UVP anchors Culture. The completed prism then feeds into Golden Circle (culture informs Why), Positioning (physique and personality inform differentiation), Tone of Voice (personality defines voice), Messaging Architecture (all facets inform messaging), and M4 Design Brief (physique informs visual direction).

## Task 1: Consolidate Brand Inputs From Prior Work

**Goal:** Assemble all relevant upstream outputs into a concise summary that prevents re-reading entire documents during facet definition.

**Inputs:**

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Brand Archetypes document (primary archetype, expression guide) | `projects/[project]/founder/brand/brand_archetypes.md` | Yes |
| JTBD analysis (emotional jobs, social jobs, forces, job map) | `projects/[project]/founder/conception/jtbd.md` | Yes |
| Working Backwards PR/FAQ (customer narrative, PR headline, customer quote) | `projects/[project]/founder/conception/working_backwards.md` | Yes |
| Lean Canvas (UVP, Customer Segments, Unfair Advantage) | `projects/[project]/founder/conception/lean_canvas.md` | Yes |
| M2 Validation findings (validated assumptions) | `projects/[project]/founder/validation/` | No |
| Problem-Solution Fit Canvas (emotions, triggers, relationship dynamics) | `projects/[project]/founder/conception/problem_solution_fit.md` | No |

**Action:**

1. From **Brand Archetypes**, extract:
   - The primary archetype name, core motivation, and one-sentence Archetype Statement.
   - Voice Character adjectives (is/is not).
   - Customer Relationship Type (named dynamic and description).
   - Visual Style Direction (mood, embrace/avoid).
   - Content Themes.
2. From **JTBD**, extract:
   - Primary emotional jobs (how customers want to feel).
   - Primary social jobs (how customers want to be perceived).
   - Key forces: push of present, pull of new, anxieties, habits.
3. From **Working Backwards**, extract:
   - PR headline and subheading (captures the brand promise in customer language).
   - Customer quote (captures the desired customer voice).
   - How It Works section (captures the tangible product experience).
4. From **Lean Canvas**, extract:
   - UVP statement.
   - Customer Segments and Early Adopters description.
   - Unfair Advantage hypothesis.
5. From **M2 Validation** (if available), extract validated insights about customer motivation, perception, and brand-relevant assumptions.
6. Compile into a **Prism Inputs Summary** (one page maximum) organised by the six facets:
   - Physique inputs: PR headline, How It Works, visual style direction, tangible product characteristics.
   - Personality inputs: Archetype name, voice character adjectives, archetype expression.
   - Culture inputs: UVP, unfair advantage, founding values, archetype core motivation.
   - Relationship inputs: Archetype relationship type, JTBD forces, Problem-Solution Fit dynamics.
   - Reflection inputs: Social jobs, customer quote, how customers want to be perceived.
   - Self-Image inputs: Emotional jobs, how customers want to feel internally.

**Output:**

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Prism Inputs Summary | One-page structured summary organised by facet | Provides a single reference document for facet definition work, preventing context loss |

**Validation:**

- [ ] Every facet has at least **two distinct inputs** from upstream artefacts.
- [ ] The summary can be read standalone without opening source documents.
- [ ] Archetype personality and voice character are present and clearly stated.
- [ ] Both emotional jobs (Self-Image) and social jobs (Reflection) are explicitly listed.

## Task 2: Define Sender Facets (Physique, Personality, Culture)

**Goal:** Define the three facets that represent what the brand sends into the world: its tangible characteristics, its character traits, and its values and beliefs.

**Inputs:**

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Prism Inputs Summary | Task 1 | Yes |
| Brand Archetypes document (full expression guide) | Applied framework | Yes |
| Lean Canvas (UVP, Unfair Advantage) | Applied framework | Yes |
| Working Backwards PR (headline, How It Works) | Applied framework | No |

**Action:**

1. **Define Physique** (tangible, visual, and product characteristics):
   - List 4-6 tangible attributes that make the brand recognisable. For a digital product, this includes: interface aesthetic (clean/dense, minimal/rich), interaction patterns (fast/deliberate, guided/exploratory), visual signature elements (colour family, typography style, spatial density), and product artefacts customers interact with (dashboards, reports, notifications, emails).
   - Do not design these elements; describe the **direction and constraints** that will guide M4 design work.
   - Write one sentence: "When someone encounters our product for the first time, they should immediately notice [attribute 1], [attribute 2], and [attribute 3]."
2. **Define Personality** (character traits the brand would have if it were a person):
   - Start with the archetype voice character adjectives from the Brand Archetypes document.
   - Expand to 5-7 personality traits that go beyond voice to include behaviour (for example, "patient" describes how the brand handles user mistakes, not just how it sounds).
   - For each trait, write one concrete behavioural example: "Because we are [trait], we [specific behaviour]."
   - List 3-5 anti-traits (what the brand personality is explicitly **not**).
3. **Define Culture** (values and beliefs the brand embodies):
   - Identify 3-5 core beliefs that drive the brand's existence beyond profit. These should connect to:
     - The archetype's core motivation (for example, the Sage believes knowledge should be accessible).
     - The Lean Canvas UVP (the belief that makes the value proposition meaningful).
     - The founding team's authentic convictions (beliefs they held before starting the company).
   - For each belief, write: "We believe [belief]. That is why we [concrete action or policy]."
   - Culture is **not** a list of corporate values like "integrity" and "innovation." It is the specific worldview that makes this brand's approach inevitable.

**Output:**

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Physique facet | Bulleted attributes plus first-impression sentence | Defines the tangible brand identity that feeds into M4 visual design |
| Personality facet | Trait list with behavioural examples, anti-traits | Defines brand character that feeds into Tone of Voice and customer experience |
| Culture facet | Belief statements with concrete manifestations | Defines brand worldview that feeds into Golden Circle Why and Messaging Architecture |

**Validation:**

- [ ] Physique attributes describe what a customer would **see and interact with**, not internal architecture.
- [ ] Personality traits are derived from the archetype and include **behavioural examples**, not just adjectives.
- [ ] Culture beliefs are **specific** enough that a competitor with different beliefs would make different product decisions.
- [ ] None of the three facets contradict each other. A brand that is physically minimal, personally patient, and culturally driven by simplicity is coherent. A brand that is physically dense, personally patient, and culturally driven by speed is not.

## Task 3: Define Receiver Facets (Reflection, Self-Image)

**Goal:** Define how the ideal customer sees themselves through the brand (Reflection) and how they feel internally when they use it (Self-Image). These are the facets that make customers feel the brand was made for them.

**Inputs:**

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Prism Inputs Summary (social jobs, emotional jobs) | Task 1 | Yes |
| JTBD analysis (emotional jobs, social jobs, customer quotes) | Applied framework | Yes |
| Working Backwards (customer quote, customer narrative) | Applied framework | Yes |
| Brand Archetypes (content themes, relationship type) | Applied framework | No |

**Action:**

1. **Define Reflection** (how the customer sees themselves when using the brand; the outward-facing mirror):
   - Reflection answers: "What kind of person uses this brand?" It is the idealised image of the customer as seen by others.
   - From JTBD social jobs, extract how customers want to be **perceived** by colleagues, managers, clients, or peers.
   - Write 3-5 Reflection statements: "A person who uses [brand] is seen as [perception]."
   - Ensure the Reflection is **aspirational but believable**. Customers should recognise themselves in the mirror, not see a stranger.
   - Important: Reflection is about the **customer's** image, not the brand's image. "Our users are innovative" is Reflection. "Our brand is innovative" is Personality. Do not confuse them.
2. **Define Self-Image** (how the customer feels internally when using the brand; the inward-facing mirror):
   - Self-Image answers: "How does using this brand make me feel about myself?"
   - From JTBD emotional jobs, extract how customers want to **feel** when doing the job your product is hired for.
   - Write 3-5 Self-Image statements: "When I use [brand], I feel [internal state]."
   - Self-Image should connect to the transformation from Problem-Solution Fit: the emotional **before** (anxious, overwhelmed, uncertain) becomes the emotional **after** (confident, in control, capable).
   - Self-Image is private. It is not about how others see the customer; it is about how the customer sees themselves.
3. Test the **distinction** between Reflection and Self-Image:
   - Reflection: "My colleagues think I am organised and ahead of the curve." (external perception)
   - Self-Image: "I feel confident that I have not missed anything important." (internal feeling)
   - If your Reflection and Self-Image statements sound the same, you have not separated the external from the internal. Revise.

**Output:**

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Reflection facet | 3-5 statements about customer's outward image | Defines how the brand helps customers project their desired social identity |
| Self-Image facet | 3-5 statements about customer's internal feeling | Defines the emotional transformation the brand delivers, feeding into messaging and experience design |

**Validation:**

- [ ] Reflection statements describe how **others** perceive the customer, not how the brand perceives itself.
- [ ] Self-Image statements describe how the **customer** feels internally, not how they appear externally.
- [ ] Each Reflection statement traces to a **social job** from JTBD.
- [ ] Each Self-Image statement traces to an **emotional job** from JTBD.
- [ ] Reflection and Self-Image are **clearly distinct** from each other. If they overlap, revise until the external/internal distinction is sharp.

## Task 4: Define Relationship Facet

**Goal:** Describe the type of relationship between brand and customer. The Relationship facet bridges sender and receiver: it is what happens in the space between the brand's character and the customer's identity.

**Inputs:**

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Brand Archetypes (relationship type from Task 4) | Applied framework | Yes |
| JTBD forces (push, pull, anxieties, habits) | Applied framework | Yes |
| Problem-Solution Fit (behaviours, constraints) | Applied framework | No |
| Prism Inputs Summary | Task 1 | Yes |

**Action:**

1. Start with the **Customer Relationship Type** defined in the Brand Archetypes document (for example, mentor-student, coach-athlete, guide-explorer, partner-partner, protector-protected).
2. Expand the relationship definition along three dimensions:
   - **What the brand promises**: What does the customer get from this relationship? (for example, "We will always explain why, not just what." "We will challenge you to improve, not just make you comfortable.")
   - **What the brand expects**: What does the customer need to bring? (for example, "You invest the time to learn." "You share honest feedback." "You trust the process.")
   - **How the relationship behaves in different moments**:
     - **Onboarding**: How does the brand welcome a new customer? (for example, a Sage brand teaches; a Hero brand challenges; a Caregiver brand nurtures.)
     - **Success**: How does the brand celebrate a customer's achievement?
     - **Failure or frustration**: How does the brand respond when something goes wrong?
     - **Departure**: How does the brand handle a customer who leaves?
3. Ground the relationship in JTBD **forces**:
   - The pull of the new solution should match what the relationship promises.
   - The anxieties should be addressed by the relationship's trust-building behaviours.
   - The habits should be respected by the relationship's pacing and expectations.
4. Write a one-paragraph **Relationship Statement** that captures the dynamic in natural language: "Our relationship with customers is like [analogy]. We [promise]. In return, we ask that they [expectation]. When things go wrong, we [crisis behaviour]."

**Output:**

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Relationship facet | Named dynamic, three-dimension expansion, moment-based behaviours, relationship statement | Defines the brand-customer dynamic that informs customer experience, support design, and communication style |

**Validation:**

- [ ] The relationship type is **named** with a clear dynamic (not just "we care about customers").
- [ ] The relationship includes both **brand promises** and **customer expectations**.
- [ ] At least **three situational moments** (onboarding, success, failure/departure) have defined brand behaviours.
- [ ] The relationship is grounded in JTBD forces: the brand addresses real anxieties and respects real habits.
- [ ] A customer support agent could read the Relationship Statement and know **how to behave** in a difficult interaction.

## Task 5: Test Consistency Across All Six Facets

**Goal:** Verify that no facet contradicts another and the prism tells a single coherent story about who the brand is.

**Inputs:**

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| All six facet definitions | Tasks 2-4 | Yes |

**Action:**

1. Write all six facets on a single page in prism layout:
   - **Top** (Sender): Physique | Personality | Culture
   - **Middle**: Relationship
   - **Bottom** (Receiver): Reflection | Self-Image
2. Read the facets as a **narrative** by answering: "Our brand looks like [Physique], acts like [Personality], believes [Culture], relates to customers as [Relationship], helps them appear [Reflection], and makes them feel [Self-Image]."
3. Test for **contradictions** between facet pairs:
   - Physique vs Personality: Does the visual identity match the character? (A playful Jester personality with a cold, corporate Physique is contradictory.)
   - Personality vs Culture: Does the character reflect the beliefs? (A rebellious Outlaw personality with a Culture of compliance is contradictory.)
   - Culture vs Relationship: Do the values support the relationship dynamic? (A Culture of radical transparency with a Relationship that withholds information is contradictory.)
   - Relationship vs Reflection: Does the relationship type produce the desired customer image? (A protective Caregiver relationship that promises to make customers look "independent and self-sufficient" is contradictory.)
   - Reflection vs Self-Image: Do the external and internal mirrors tell a compatible story? (Customers who appear "cutting-edge" to others but feel "confused and overwhelmed" internally is contradictory.)
   - Physique vs Self-Image: Does the tangible experience deliver the internal feeling? (A cluttered, dense Physique that promises customers will feel "calm and in control" is contradictory.)
4. For each contradiction found, identify which facet is the outlier and **revise it** to align with the majority.
5. Perform a **competitor contrast test**: read your complete prism narrative, then read what you imagine a competitor's prism would say. If the two are interchangeable, your prism is too generic. Sharpen the facets that lack distinctiveness.
6. Document all revisions and the reasoning behind them.

**Output:**

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Consistency assessment | Narrative summary of cross-facet tests performed and results | Proves the prism is coherent, not a collection of disconnected statements |
| Revision notes | Bulleted list of any facet changes made and why | Documents the reasoning behind adjustments for future reference |

**Validation:**

- [ ] You have tested at least **six facet pairs** for contradictions.
- [ ] The full prism narrative reads as a **single coherent character**, not a collage.
- [ ] Any contradictions found have been **resolved** with documented reasoning.
- [ ] The prism is **distinct** from what a major competitor's prism would say.

## Task 6: Document Prism And Wire Into Downstream Frameworks

**Goal:** Save the completed Brand Prism as a canonical artefact and explicitly connect each facet to downstream frameworks.

**Inputs:**

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Consistent six-facet Brand Prism | Tasks 2-5 | Yes |
| Brand process doc | `brand_process.md` | Yes |
| M3 founder diary | `projects/[project]/founder/brand/m3_founder_diary.md` | Yes |

**Action:**

1. Compile the final **Brand Prism document** with these sections:
   - Prism Inputs Summary (from Task 1).
   - All six facet definitions with supporting evidence and examples.
   - Consistency assessment and any revision notes.
   - Prism narrative (the single-sentence story from Task 5).
   - Integration notes (see step 3 below).
2. Save the document in `projects/[project]/founder/brand/brand_prism.md`.
3. Write explicit **integration notes** for each downstream framework:
   - **Golden Circle**: Culture facet informs the Why. The brand's core beliefs are the foundation of its purpose. The Personality facet's values orientation also supports the How.
   - **Brand Positioning**: Physique and Personality together inform differentiation. The tangible characteristics and character traits should be reflected in the positioning statement's "unlike [competitor]" clause.
   - **Tone of Voice**: Personality facet defines the voice. The 5-7 personality traits with behavioural examples become the starting point for tone dimensions and do/don't guidelines.
   - **Messaging Architecture**: All six facets inform messaging. Physique and Culture feed brand-level messages. Reflection and Self-Image feed customer-facing benefit messages. Relationship defines the overall messaging tone and stance.
   - **M4 Design Brief**: Physique facet informs visual identity direction. The tangible attributes, first-impression sentence, and colour/typography/spatial direction provide constraints for design exploration.
4. Update the **M3 founder diary** with:
   - The completed prism summary (one sentence per facet).
   - Key decisions and trade-offs made during facet definition.
   - Any facets that required revision during the consistency test and why.
5. Update **M3 founder diary** status to reflect Brand Prism completion.

**Output:**

| Output | Format | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Final Brand Prism document | Markdown in `projects/[project]/founder/brand/` | Canonical brand identity reference for all downstream brand and design work |
| Integration notes | Section within prism document | Explicit wiring instructions for Golden Circle, Positioning, Tone of Voice, Messaging Architecture, and M4 Design Brief |
| M3 founder diary entry | Row(s) in diary | Captures prism decisions, trade-offs, and revision reasoning |

**Validation:**

- [ ] The Brand Prism document contains all **six facets** with definitions, evidence, and examples.
- [ ] The consistency assessment shows **no unresolved contradictions** between facets.
- [ ] Integration notes explicitly name what each downstream framework receives.
- [ ] The M3 founder diary has at least one entry documenting the prism decisions.
- [ ] The prism narrative can be read aloud in **under 30 seconds** and a listener would understand the brand's identity.

## Pitfalls

**Confusing Personality With Reflection**

Personality describes **the brand's character** (how the brand behaves). Reflection describes **how the customer appears** to others through the brand (how the customer is perceived). "We are innovative" is Personality. "Our users are seen as innovative" is Reflection. Mixing these produces facets that all sound like brand self-description, leaving the customer invisible.

**Instead:** For every Reflection statement, verify the subject is the **customer**, not the brand. The test: "Would the customer's colleague say this about the customer because they use our brand?"

---

**Treating Physique As Just A Logo Description**

For digital products, Physique is far more than visual identity. It includes interaction patterns, information density, notification cadence, email formatting, and every tangible element the customer encounters. Reducing Physique to "blue logo, clean design" produces a facet that cannot guide M4 design work.

**Instead:** Define Physique as the **full sensory experience** of interacting with the brand. Include interface characteristics, communication artefacts, and product feel. The first-impression sentence should describe what a customer notices when they **use** the product, not just when they see the logo.

---

**Defining Culture As Generic Corporate Values**

Writing Culture as "integrity, innovation, customer-centricity" produces a facet indistinguishable from any other brand. Every company claims these values. Culture in the prism must capture the **specific worldview** that makes this brand's decisions inevitable.

**Instead:** Frame Culture as beliefs: "We believe [specific claim about the world]. That is why we [specific action no generic company would take]." If a competitor could copy your Culture statement without changing their behaviour, it is too generic. Rewrite.

---

**Skipping The Consistency Test**

Filling six facets separately without testing them against each other produces a prism full of internal contradictions. A playful Personality with a formal Physique. A Culture of transparency with a Relationship that gatekeeps information. These contradictions become visible to customers even if they are invisible to the founding team.

**Instead:** Complete Task 5 in full. Test at least six facet pairs for contradictions. Read the complete prism narrative aloud and listen for dissonance. If any facet does not belong in the same story as the others, revise it.

---

**Building The Prism Without The Archetype**

Starting the prism from scratch without the Brand Archetypes analysis means the Personality facet is invented rather than derived. This disconnects Personality from the emotional territory established in Step 2 and forces you to redo archetype-equivalent reasoning inside the prism with less rigour.

**Instead:** MUST complete Brand Archetypes before starting the prism. The Personality facet should begin with the archetype's voice character and expand from there, not be created independently.

---

**Ignoring Self-Image In Favour Of Reflection**

Teams frequently over-invest in Reflection (how customers want to be perceived) because it feels closer to marketing messaging, while neglecting Self-Image (how customers feel internally). Self-Image is where the deepest brand loyalty forms. Customers who feel confident, calm, or capable because of your brand will not switch to a competitor over features.

**Instead:** Give Self-Image equal weight to Reflection. Ground every Self-Image statement in a validated emotional job from JTBD. Test by asking: "When a customer uses our product alone with no one watching, how do they feel?" That answer is Self-Image.

## Integration

**Prerequisites:**
Read `brand_process.md`. MUST complete Brand Archetypes (M3 Step 2). MUST have access to M1 Conception artefacts (JTBD, Working Backwards, Lean Canvas) and M2 Validation findings.

**Builds On:**

| Framework | Output Used | How Used |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Brand Archetypes | Primary archetype personality, voice character, relationship type, visual style direction, content themes | Personality facet starts from archetype traits; Relationship facet starts from archetype relationship type; Physique facet uses visual style direction |
| Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD) | Emotional jobs, social jobs, forces (push, pull, anxieties, habits) | Self-Image derives from emotional jobs; Reflection derives from social jobs; Relationship facet addresses anxieties and habits |
| Working Backwards (PR/FAQ) | Customer narrative, PR headline, customer quote, How It Works | Physique uses tangible product description; Reflection uses customer aspiration language; overall prism uses customer voice |
| Lean Canvas | UVP, Customer Segments, Unfair Advantage | Culture facet anchors in UVP and unfair advantage; Physique considers segment expectations |
| M2 Validation | Validated assumptions about customer motivation and perception | All facets use validated rather than hypothetical customer insights where available |

**Feeds Into:**

| Framework | Output Provided | How Used |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Golden Circle (Sinek) | Culture facet (beliefs and values) | Culture informs the Why; the brand's worldview becomes its stated purpose |
| Brand Positioning | Physique + Personality (tangible characteristics and character) | These facets inform the differentiation clause of the positioning statement |
| Tone of Voice Guidelines | Personality facet (traits with behavioural examples) | Personality traits become tone dimensions with do/don't guidance |
| Messaging Architecture | All six facets | Physique and Culture feed brand-level messages; Reflection and Self-Image feed benefit messages; Relationship defines messaging stance |
| M4 Design Brief | Physique facet (visual direction, interaction characteristics) | Physique attributes and first-impression sentence constrain visual identity exploration |

**Workflow Position:**

Brand Prism sits in M3 as Step 3, directly after Brand Archetypes (Step 2) and before Golden Circle (Step 4). It is the central identity document that synthesises archetype personality, JTBD emotional territory, and business model inputs into a complete brand identity system.

## Success Criteria

- [ ] All **six facets** (Physique, Personality, Culture, Relationship, Reflection, Self-Image) are defined with specific, evidence-based statements.
- [ ] The **Personality facet** is derived from the Brand Archetypes analysis, not invented independently.
- [ ] The **Reflection facet** traces to JTBD social jobs and describes the customer's outward image, not the brand's self-image.
- [ ] The **Self-Image facet** traces to JTBD emotional jobs and describes how the customer feels internally.
- [ ] The **Relationship facet** includes brand promises, customer expectations, and situational behaviours (onboarding, success, failure).
- [ ] The **consistency test** has been completed across at least six facet pairs with no unresolved contradictions.
- [ ] The complete prism narrative can be read in under 30 seconds and conveys a coherent brand identity.
- [ ] The Brand Prism document is saved in the project brand folder.
- [ ] Integration notes explicitly state what Golden Circle, Positioning, Tone of Voice, Messaging Architecture, and M4 Design Brief receive from the prism.
- [ ] The M3 founder diary contains at least one entry documenting prism decisions and trade-offs.

## For AI Agents

**Execution Context:**

1. Read `brand_process.md` and confirm that Brand Archetypes (M3 Step 2) is complete before starting. If it is not complete, MUST execute Brand Archetypes first.
2. Confirm that M1 artefacts (JTBD, Working Backwards, Lean Canvas) are accessible. The prism cannot be built without them.
3. Track progress explicitly: "Currently in Task [N]: [Task Name]".
4. Confirm each task's validation checklist before proceeding to the next task.
5. When defining Reflection and Self-Image, force the distinction between external perception and internal feeling. If the user conflates them, provide corrective examples.
6. During the consistency test (Task 5), systematically check each facet pair rather than relying on a holistic impression.
7. Record prism decisions and trade-offs in the M3 founder diary as soon as Task 5 is complete.

**Working With Tasks:**

- Complete each task atomically. Do not define multiple facets simultaneously; the order (Sender, then Receiver, then Relationship) is deliberate because each layer builds on the previous.
- If the user lacks a Brand Archetypes document, do not improvise the Personality facet. Flag this as a blocker and recommend completing Brand Archetypes first.
- If the consistency test reveals contradictions, do not dismiss them. Guide the user through identifying the outlier facet and revising it with explicit reasoning.
- When preparing integration notes, be specific about what each downstream framework receives. "Brand Prism feeds into Tone of Voice" is insufficient. "Personality traits with behavioural examples become the starting point for tone dimensions" is what is needed.
