# Jobs-to-be-Done: Robotville v4.0

## Job Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: The Idea That Won't Go Away
**Type:** Functional

**Job Statement:** When I keep coming back to a business idea but don't know how to evaluate whether it's real or just excitement, I want to systematically stress-test it against proven frameworks, so I can either commit with confidence or kill it early before wasting months and money.

**Validation Need:** Do aspiring founders actually try to evaluate ideas before building — or do they just build (or do nothing)? Is "kill early" a recognized need, or only obvious in hindsight?

### Hypothesis 2: The Methodology Gap
**Type:** Functional

**Job Statement:** When I decide to actually pursue a business idea but don't have startup experience or know what steps to take, I want structured step-by-step guidance through the thinking that comes before building, so I can go from raw idea to a validated concept with a defined customer, competitive position, and business model.

**Validation Need:** Do non-technical founders recognize the "thinking before building" gap — or do they jump straight to execution tools (Lovable, ChatGPT, freelancers)? Is "I don't know the steps" articulated as a pain point?

### Hypothesis 3: Ready to Launch, Not Just Ready to Pitch
**Type:** Functional

**Job Statement:** When I've validated my business concept and need to actually bring it to market, I want to go from documented strategy to a real brand identity and deployable website/app, so I can launch the business — not just have a plan for one.

**Validation Need:** Is the gap between "validated concept" and "launched business" felt as a distinct pain point — or do users expect the strategy tool and the building tool to be separate? Does end-to-end (thinking → launch) matter?

### Hypothesis 4: The Honest Mirror
**Type:** Emotional

**Job Statement:** When I'm excited about my idea and surrounded by people who say "great idea!" but nobody who challenges it, I want brutally honest, structured pushback on my assumptions, so I can trust that what survives the process is actually worth building.

**Validation Need:** Do aspiring founders actively seek critical feedback — or avoid it? Is "AI that pushes back" attractive or threatening to the target user?

### Forces Analysis

| Force | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Push of Present** | Ideas die in heads. Google → templates, jargon, blank canvases. ChatGPT gives answers but no structure, no pushback, no methodology. Consultants cost $5K+. Vibe coding tools build the wrong thing without business thinking. **However:** the push is weaker than initially assumed — many users feel *indifference* not frustration. The idea is a pleasant daydream, not an urgent problem. The push only activates for those who cross from curiosity to intent (a smaller but more motivated subset). | Founder experience; WB alternatives analysis; PSF Party Mode challenge (John) |
| **Pull of New** | Chat-based (already use messaging daily). AI that guides AND challenges. Goes from idea to launched business, not just a plan. No technical skills needed. Available when the idea strikes. **Refined pull:** the core emotional pull is *clarity and permission to act* — not rigorous thinking. Users want to feel ready, not analytically exhaustive. | WB press release; testimonial scenario; PSF Party Mode challenge (Maya) |
| **Anxieties** | "Will AI actually understand my specific idea?" "Can a chat conversation really replace human expertise?" "Is this just another AI gimmick?" "What if I invest time and the output is generic?" | Founder hypothesis — needs M2 validation |
| **Habits** | **The comfort of inaction is the #1 competitor.** Doing nothing is not a failure mode — it's a competing product with real benefits: zero risk, social validation from retelling the idea, identity as "the person with the idea" without exposure to failure. The social validation loop (talking to friends who say "great idea!") delivers emotional satisfaction that actively substitutes for structured development. Other habits: Googling and giving up. Jumping straight to building with vibe coding tools without business thinking. | WB current alternatives analysis; PSF Party Mode challenge (Victor) |

**Adoption condition:** Push + Pull > Anxieties + Habits. **Revised assessment (post-PSF Party Mode):** The push is *moderate* — methodology is genuinely inaccessible, but most people with ideas don't feel the pain strongly enough to act. The pull is strong but must be reframed: *clarity and permission to act*, not rigorous analytical thinking. The key risk is not just anxieties — it's **habits**. The comfort of inaction and the social validation loop are the strongest forces keeping users from switching. The first interaction must not only prove AI can do this but must feel *immediately more rewarding* than talking about the idea with friends.

### Primary Job

**Statement:** When I decide to actually pursue a business idea but don't have startup experience or know what steps to take, I want structured step-by-step guidance through the thinking that comes before building, so I can go from raw idea to a validated concept with a defined customer, competitive position, and business model.

**Why This Job:** This is the core of what Robotville does — it IS the methodology. The 22 frameworks across 6 milestones are the structured guidance. Every other job (stress-testing ideas, honest feedback, launching) depends on this methodology layer existing and being accessible. Current alternatives either don't provide methodology (ChatGPT, friends), are too expensive (consultants), or skip the thinking entirely (vibe coding tools). The satisfaction gap is enormous.

### Key Assumptions

1. Non-technical users will recognize the "thinking before building" gap and seek structured methodology (vs. jumping to execution tools)
2. Chat-based delivery is sufficient for complex multi-step business frameworks — users won't abandon mid-journey
3. AI-driven pushback (critical=10, sycophancy=1) is perceived as valuable, not threatening, by the target user
4. **[Added post-PSF Party Mode]** A meaningful percentage of people with business ideas will cross the threshold from curiosity/daydreaming to active intent — i.e., the addressable segment is not "everyone with ideas" but "people who've decided to act"
5. **[Added post-PSF Party Mode]** The first interaction with Robotville delivers more immediate emotional reward than talking about the idea with friends (must beat the social validation loop)
