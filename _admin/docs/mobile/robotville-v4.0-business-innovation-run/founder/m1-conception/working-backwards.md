# Working Backwards: Robotville v4.0

## Customer & Problem Brief

**Primary Customer:**
Aspiring first-time business creators — professionals (25-50) with domain expertise in their field but no startup experience, limited technical sophistication, and no formal training in business innovation or strategy.

**Their Context:**
These are people who have business ideas constantly — at a bar with friends, on the bus, during a long trip — but have never built a business before. They are not developers. They are not startup veterans. They see AI transforming industries and want to benefit from it, but when they use ChatGPT or similar tools, they use them as search engines — they don't know how to prompt strategically, and the AI doesn't push back or guide them through structured business thinking. They don't know what a Lean Canvas is, let alone how to fill one out. They have the ideas but lack the methodology, the frameworks, and the structured guidance to turn a "what if" into a validated concept with a brand, customer definition, and prototype.

**Emotional reality (refined post-PSF):** These customers are not frustrated or in pain — they are *curious and uncertain.* The business idea is a pleasant recurring thought, not an urgent problem. Most never feel enough discomfort to act. The subset that crosses from daydreaming to active intent is small but highly motivated — triggered by a specific decision moment (career disruption, life milestone, competitive fear), not accumulated frustration. They are stalled at the starting line, not paralyzed. The emotional "before" is curiosity mixed with uncertainty about whether they belong; the desired "after" is clarity and permission to act, not rigorous analytical output.

**Brazil Market Context (Primary):**
Brazil is the initial target market and an ideal testing ground. 60% of Brazilian users access the internet exclusively via mobile. WhatsApp has 144M social media identities in the country — it is the dominant communication channel. Pix processes 250.5M daily transactions; Pix Automatico (launched Jun 2025) enables recurring payments, projected to drive $30B in e-commerce in its first 2 years. This infrastructure means: WhatsApp delivery is not a nice-to-have — it's how the primary market accesses the internet. And Pix Automatico provides a native subscription billing channel with zero friction.

**Problem Statement:**
People with business ideas have no accessible, structured way to develop those ideas into real businesses. The business innovation skills, frameworks, and methodology needed to go from raw idea to validated concept are locked behind expensive consultants, MBA programs, or tools that require technical sophistication the target user doesn't have.

**Examples in the Wild:**
1. A marketing manager with 15 years of industry experience who has a niche SaaS idea but has never written a business plan or validated a market
2. A restaurant owner who wants to launch a new delivery concept but doesn't know how to structure, validate, or articulate the idea beyond "I think people would pay for this"
3. A corporate professional who sees AI reshaping their industry and wants to start something but has no framework for thinking through customer, problem, solution, and business model

**Current Alternatives:**
- **Do nothing + the social validation loop (strongest competitor)** — Most people with ideas never act on them, and *this is not experienced as failure.* Inaction is a comfort zone with real benefits: zero risk, social validation from retelling the idea, identity as "the person with the idea" without exposure to failure. Talking to friends who say "great idea!" delivers emotional satisfaction that actively substitutes for structured development. Robotville's real competition is not bad tools — it's the comfort of never committing. *(Insight added post-PSF Party Mode — Victor)*
- **ChatGPT / AI chatbots** — Unstructured. No business frameworks. No pushback. Most people use them as search engines, not thinking partners. No guided methodology.
- **Business consultants** — Expensive ($5K+), inaccessible on-demand, not available at the moment the idea strikes.
- **AI-based business platforms / courses** — Often misleading, selling promises without delivering real structured innovation methodology.
- **Lovable / vibe coding tools** — For the more curious or technical users. Can build apps and websites, but does none of the business thinking — no customer validation, no competitive analysis, no brand strategy. Building without thinking.
- **Google + blank templates** — Lean Canvas PDFs, blog posts, YouTube tutorials. No guidance, no feedback, no iteration. The user stares at a blank template and gives up.

## External FAQ

**Q1. What is Robotville?**
Robotville is an AI-powered business innovation platform that guides you from a raw idea to a validated business concept — with a brand, customer definition, competitive analysis, and even a working prototype. You interact with it entirely through messaging apps you already use (Slack now, WhatsApp planned Q1 2026).

**Q2. How does it work?**
You start a conversation and type "mentor." An AI mentor agent greets you, asks about your idea, and walks you through a structured sequence of business frameworks — the same ones used by top accelerators and strategy consultants. Each framework builds on the previous one. The agent challenges your thinking, asks hard questions, and refuses to let you skip steps. You can also type "domcobb" for help structuring a problem or articulating something you can't yet put into words.

**Q3. Do I need any technical skills?**
No. If you can type a message, you can use Robotville. There is no code, no IDE, no setup. The agents guide you conversationally.

**Q4. Is this just ChatGPT with a business prompt?**
No. ChatGPT is a general-purpose tool with no structure or pushback. Robotville runs 22 specialized business frameworks in a specific sequence, with an opinionated AI mentor that challenges weak thinking. It's the difference between having a blank conversation and having a structured program with milestones, deliverables, and quality gates.

**Q5. What do I get at the end?**
After completing all 6 milestones, you have: a documented business concept, brand identity, competitive landscape analysis, validated assumptions, and a deployable project website at `robotville.ai/app/{your-project}`. **[ASSUMPTION — website deployment pipeline not yet built; requires validation]**

**Q7. Is this for "startups" specifically?**
No. The frameworks apply to any new business — a restaurant concept, a freelance service, a local delivery operation, a digital product. "Business innovation" is not limited to tech startups.

**Q8. What messaging platforms do you support?**
Slack at launch. WhatsApp integration planned for Q1 2026.

## Internal FAQ

### Feasibility and Resources

**Q1. What capabilities must we build that we don't have today?**
The core innovation system (RBTV) already exists and works in Cursor. What's new is the delivery layer: Nanobot runtime + Slack integration + bootstrap files (`_mobile/` folder). Estimated build: 2 days for prototype. The AI agent tools (read/write files, shell exec, web search) map directly to Nanobot's built-in tools.

**Q2. What are the top technical unknowns?**
1. **Per-user memory and personality isolation** — Nanobot currently runs single-tenant (session keyed to `channel:chat_id`). For a real multi-user product, each user needs isolated conversation history, project state, and agent memory. Architecture for this is not designed yet.
2. **Context window survival** — Nanobot auto-consolidates after 50 messages (LLM summarizes old messages into MEMORY.md + HISTORY.md, trims to last ~10-25 messages). Long frameworks may lose mid-step context. Mitigation exists: RBTV's `project-memo.md` frontmatter is external state that persists regardless of consolidation, and each step file is self-contained. SOUL.md bootstrap includes rule to always read project-memo before responding. Also considering `memory_window: 100` for richer retention.
3. **No MCP support in Nanobot** — RBTV uses Playwright MCP from M3 (Brand) onward for browser automation. Options: (a) skip browser workflows in prototype, (b) wrap Playwright CLI via shell exec, (c) write a custom Nanobot tool. Not blocking M1-M2.

**Q3. How does the technical architecture work?**
Nanobot loads bootstrap files into the system prompt on every call: `AGENTS.md` (agent persona routing — mentor, domcobb, etc.), `SOUL.md` (RBTV core rules, "always read project-memo before responding"), `TOOLS.md` (command-to-workflow mapping), `USER.md` (user preferences). A skills system (`workspace/skills/{name}/SKILL.md`) auto-summarizes available skills in the system prompt — directly compatible with RBTV's existing `.cursor/skills/` pattern. Memory is two-layer: `MEMORY.md` (long-term facts, always in system prompt) + `HISTORY.md` (grep-searchable log), auto-consolidated from conversation. The `_mobile/` folder inside `_bmad/rbtv/` contains the adapter layer with all bootstrap files, a config template, and an `install.py` script that sets up a VPS workspace from the existing RBTV codebase (including a self-contained BMAD mirror).

**Q4. What's the team situation?**
Two founders with IB, corporate development (M&A, VC, strategic alliances), and previous startup experience. One engineer engaged informally — testing the system and evaluating whether to join the team. No other hires yet.

### Dependencies and Risks

**Q8. What external dependencies could block us?**
- **AI provider pricing** — API cost changes directly impact unit economics
- **Slack/WhatsApp platform policies** — bot restrictions or policy changes could limit distribution
- **Nanobot maintenance** — small open-source project (~4K lines); if abandoned, we'd need to fork or migrate

**Q9. What would make you kill this project after 3-6 months?**
- Users consistently abandon before completing the first milestone (M1)
- Per-user isolation proves architecturally infeasible without a full rewrite
- AI API costs make unit economics impossible at any reasonable price point
- No evidence of willingness to pay after 100+ free users

### Strategic Fit

**Q10. Why now?**
The pace of AI improvement is measurable and accelerating. METR (an organization that tracks the length of real-world tasks AI can complete end-to-end without human help) shows task duration doubling approximately every seven months — and recent data suggests it may be accelerating to every four months. A year ago, models could handle ~10-minute tasks. By late 2025, they were completing tasks that take a human expert nearly five hours. Amodei has said models "substantially smarter than almost all humans at almost all tasks" are on track for 2026-2027 ([source: Matt Shumer, "How fast this is actually moving"](https://x.com/mattshumer_/article/2021256989876109403)). The macro data supports the timing: 473K new business applications per month in the US (Census, Aug 2025), no-code market projected at ~$50B by 2028 (Forrester), The Economist writing about one-person unicorns (Aug 2025), and $1.4T processed by Stripe in 2024 (+38% YoY). AI capability is crossing the threshold where it can handle full business innovation workflows — structured questioning, framework execution, document generation — in a single session. ChatGPT made conversational AI normal. Vibe coding tools made building accessible. But nobody has made business *thinking* accessible through the same channel. AI knows how to build — it doesn't know what to build. That gap is widening as the tools on both sides get better, and Robotville sits directly in it.

**Q11. The Press Release uses pain-point language — does that conflict with the PSF-corrected emotional model?**
Yes, deliberately. The PR says "ideas die in people's heads, not because of lack of ambition, but because of lack of methodology" — this frames the problem as loss. PSF analysis (completed later) revealed the actual customer emotional state is curiosity mixed with uncertainty, not frustration or loss. For most, the idea is a pleasant daydream — they're stalled, not suffering. The PR language is retained as effective marketing copy (pain framing converts better in press and pitch contexts), but all downstream work — M3 Brand, M4 Prototypation, onboarding design — must use the PSF-corrected emotional model. The customer doesn't need pain relieved; they need momentum created.

### Is It Worth Doing?

**Yes — with caveats.**

**Rationale:** The product concept is sound — structured business innovation via messaging is a genuine gap. The prototype is low-risk (2 days, existing codebase, no new infrastructure beyond a VPS). The VC demo purpose is clear: demonstrate AI competence and product thinking, not revenue. The long-term bet is that making RBTV accessible outside Cursor unlocks a market 100x larger than developer-tool users.

**Key risks to validate in M2:**
1. Will non-technical users actually complete a multi-step innovation framework through chat?
2. Can per-user isolation be solved without rebuilding the runtime?
3. Is there willingness to pay, and at what price point?
4. Does context window consolidation break long workflows?
5. Can the first interaction beat the comfort of inaction? The social validation loop (friends who say "great idea!") and the psychological reward of never committing are the strongest competing alternative — not bad tools. If Robotville's first 5 minutes don't deliver more immediate reward than the bar napkin conversation, users default back to inaction. (See PSF-2.)

## Synthesis

**Primary Customer:** Aspiring first-time business creators — professionals (25-50) with domain expertise but no startup experience, limited technical sophistication, who have ideas constantly but lack the methodology to develop them.

**Core Problem:** The business innovation skills, frameworks, and methodology needed to go from raw idea to validated concept are locked behind expensive consultants, MBA programs, or tools that require technical sophistication the target user doesn't have.
