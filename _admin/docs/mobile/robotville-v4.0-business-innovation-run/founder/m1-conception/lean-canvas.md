# Lean Canvas: Robotville v4.0

## Canvas Brief

**Lean Canvas v0.1 — Idea Stage**

**Scope:** AI-guided business innovation delivered through messaging apps. Single segment (aspiring first-time business creators), single situation (the gap between having an idea and knowing how to develop it).

**Stage:** Pre-revenue. No users outside founders. All blocks are hypotheses for M2 validation.

## 1. Problem

| # | Problem Statement | Frequency | Severity |
|---|-------------------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | "I have this business idea but I don't know where to start or what steps to take" | Every time the idea resurfaces (weekly+) | High — idea dies by attrition, opportunity lost |
| 2 | "Everything I try either gives me generic advice or wants $5,000" | Every attempt to get help | High — gives up on structured approach entirely |
| 3 | "I keep talking about it but nothing actually happens" | Ongoing (weeks/months) | High — months pass, idea never materializes, social validation substitutes for progress |

**Existing Alternatives:**
- Do nothing + social validation loop (friends say "great idea!")
- ChatGPT / AI chatbots (generic, no structure, no pushback)
- Google + blank templates (overwhelming, vocabulary barrier)
- Business consultants ($5K+, inaccessible on-demand)
- Vibe coding tools (build without thinking — skip the "what" and jump to "how")

**Source traceability:**
- P1 ← JTBD H2 (methodology gap) + PSF situation #7 (blank template wall)
- P2 ← PSF situations #8 (ChatGPT dead end) + #10 (consultant sticker shock)
- P3 ← PSF situation #6 (bar napkin moment) + JTBD habits (social validation loop)

## 2. Customer Segments

**Primary Segment:**
Aspiring first-time business creators (25-50) with domain expertise in their field, no startup experience, limited technical sophistication — at the moment they cross from daydreaming to active intent.

**Context:**
They've decided to pursue the idea but don't know the steps. The trigger was specific — career disruption, life milestone, competitive fear, or growing curiosity that shifted from passive to active. They are not frustrated; they are curious and uncertain. They want clarity and permission to act, not rigorous analysis.

**Early Adopters:**
People who have already crossed the activation threshold — specifically those experiencing career disruption (layoff, restructuring, burnout) or a life milestone that created urgency. They have fewer competing priorities (or their priorities just changed), higher motivation to act now, and are actively searching for "how to start a business" rather than just thinking about it.

**Examples in the Wild:**
1. Marketing manager (15 years experience) with a niche SaaS idea — never written a business plan or validated a market
2. Restaurant owner who wants to launch a new delivery concept — can describe the idea but can't structure, validate, or articulate it beyond "I think people would pay for this"
3. Corporate professional who sees AI reshaping their industry — wants to start something but has no framework for thinking through customer, problem, solution, and business model

## 3. Unique Value Proposition

**One-Sentence UVP:**
For **aspiring first-time business creators** who don't know where to start, can't find structured help they can afford, and keep talking about their idea without acting, **Robotville** is an **AI-powered business innovation mentor** that **turns a chat conversation into a structured business concept — from raw idea to validated plan to deployed product**, unlike **ChatGPT** which gives generic advice with no methodology or pushback, and **business consultants** who cost $5K+ and aren't available when the idea strikes.

**Differentiation Basis:**
- vs ChatGPT: Robotville runs 22 structured frameworks in sequence with an opinionated mentor that challenges weak thinking. ChatGPT generates text; Robotville generates progress.
- vs Business consultants: Same methodology, 100x cheaper, available at midnight on the bus, accessible through a chat message.
- vs Vibe coding tools (Lovable): Robotville makes you think before you build. Same system that validates the concept also guides the build — no gap between plan and product.
- vs Do nothing / social validation loop: First conversation produces a structured artefact in 20 minutes. Replaces "great idea!" with "who have you sold it to?" while preserving the conversational format.

**High-Concept Pitch:**
"YC mentor in your pocket — through a chat message."

**Source traceability:**
- UVP headline ← WB PR headline ("Napkin to Reality — Through a Simple Chat Message")
- Core mechanism ← PSF ("social validation loop → progress loop")
- End-to-end pipeline ← PSF behaviours changed table

## 4. Solution

**Top 3 Features/Capabilities:**

| # | Solution Element | Solves | Customer Change |
|---|------------------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | AI Mentor agent with 22 structured business frameworks in guided sequence | P1 ("don't know where to start") | Customer goes from "I have an idea" to "I have a documented business concept with defined customer, problem, solution, and business model" — through conversation, not forms |
| 2 | Opinionated pushback that replaces validation with progress | P3 ("keep talking, nothing happens") | Each conversation produces a concrete artefact, not just a good feeling. Progress loop replaces social validation loop |
| 3 | End-to-end thinking→building pipeline (M1-M6) via messaging, no code required | P2 ("generic advice or $5K") | Same system that structures the thinking also builds the product — from napkin to deployed website at `robotville.ai/app/{project}` for ~$10/mo + optional deployment fee |

**Integration Considerations:**
- Delivered via messaging apps users already use (Slack now, WhatsApp planned) — zero onboarding friction
- Self-serve from first message. No account creation, no download, no credit card
- State persists via project-memo — works in 20-30 minute fragments on the bus, at night, during lunch

## 6. Revenue Streams

**Dual Revenue Model: Subscription + Deployment Fee**

| Revenue Stream | Type | Price Hypothesis | Trigger |
|----------------|------|------------------|---------|
| **Free tier** | Freemium | $0 | First message. 1 project, full M1 access. Site hosted at `robotville.ai/app/{project}` |
| **Subscription** | Monthly | ~$10/month | After M1 completion. Unlocks additional projects, M2-M6 access, priority AI |
| **Custom domain deployment** | One-time | $30-50 | User wants to move site from `robotville.ai/app/...` to their own domain. Charged at moment of highest perceived value |

**Rationale:**
- **Subscription at ~$10/mo**: Under the "not serious enough to spend" threshold for the segment. Pix Automatico-friendly for Brazil. 500x cheaper than consultants. Competitive with AI tool subscriptions ($10-30/mo range).
- **Deployment fee at $30-50**: Completion premium — charged at the exact moment the user values the output most (their idea is built, they want to go live). Still 10-50x cheaper than hiring someone to deploy a site. Impulse-purchase territory.
- **Free tier with `robotville.ai` hosting**: Every free site is a live portfolio piece for Robotville. Users who never pay for custom domain are still marketing for you.

**Key Revenue Assumptions:**
- Willingness to pay: ~$10/month after free M1 experience — updated from PSF-9 ($15-50 → $10 based on founder market knowledge)
- Deployment conversion: X% of M6 completers pay $30-50 for custom domain — needs validation
- Expected free→paid conversion: 5-10% of M1 completers (hypothesis)

## 7. Cost Structure

**Fixed Costs:**

| Cost | Monthly Estimate | Notes |
|------|------------------|-------|
| Founder time | $0 (bootstrapping) | 2 founders, no salaries initially |
| VPS hosting | $50-150 | Nanobot + BMAD instance, single server |
| Domain (robotville.ai) | ~$2 | Already owned |
| Slack workspace | $0 | Free tier sufficient for bot |

**Variable Costs (per customer):**

| Cost | Per-Unit Estimate | Driver |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| AI API calls | $2-5 per framework session | Maps to PSF-10. ~$15-30 per full M1-M6 journey |
| VPS compute per concurrent user | ~$1-3/month | Scales with simultaneous sessions |

**Step Functions:**
- VPS upgrade at ~50 concurrent users
- Per-user isolation architecture before open beta (PSF-7)
- First hire (community/support) at ~200 paying users

**Path to Positive Unit Economics:**
At $10/month + $40 avg deployment fee, a user who completes full journey over 4 months generates ~$80 revenue vs ~$20-35 in API costs. Gross margin ~55-75%. Tight but viable if API costs trend down (likely). At 100 paying customers = $1,000 MRR + deployment fees. Break-even on founders' time requires ~1,000+ paying users. **Unit economics are the #1 risk at this price point — must validate API costs in M2.**

**Unit Economics Sanity Check:**
- LTV hypothesis: $10/month x 4 months + $40 deployment = $80 (conservative)
- CAC hypothesis: Product-led, organic = $5-15 (low-touch)
- LTV:CAC ratio: 5-16x — viable if retention and completion hold
- **Risk**: If API costs exceed $5/framework, margins collapse. Cheaper models and caching are the primary mitigation

## 9. Unfair Advantage

**Honest assessment: No moat today. Building toward one.**

**Primary Advantage (building toward):**
Compound methodology + structured business data + Brazil distribution

**Type:** Proprietary data + content moat + geographic distribution

**Defensibility Thesis:**
- **22 frameworks across 6 milestones** — months of design, testing, and iteration in BMAD/RBTV. A competitor can't copy the system by reading a landing page. They'd need to replicate the entire methodology architecture, agent personas, quality gates, and cross-framework traceability. That's months of work even with a well-funded team.
- **Structured business data** — as users complete frameworks, Robotville accumulates a unique dataset: real business ideas, real customer definitions, real competitive analyses, real assumption inventories. Over time, this improves AI pushback quality, benchmark data, and framework suggestions. Every user makes the system smarter for the next user.
- **`robotville.ai/app/{project}` portfolio** — every deployed project is a live case study. The gallery grows organically. More projects = more social proof = more trust = more users. Mild network effect.
- **Brazil distribution** — WhatsApp (144M users) + Pix Automatico (native recurring payments) = a distribution channel that international competitors can't replicate without local presence.

**Current State:** Building toward moat, don't have one yet. The methodology exists but hasn't been battle-tested with real users.

**Time to Moat:** 6-12 months of user data + 100+ completed projects in the gallery.

**Vulnerability:** A well-funded AI company (ChatGPT, etc.) could add structured business frameworks as a feature. Mitigation: too niche for their roadmap. The real risk is a direct competitor forking BMAD (it's open source).

## Synthesis

### Canvas Summary

**Version:** Lean Canvas v0.1 — Idea Stage (Pre-product)

**Business Model Hypothesis:**
Robotville delivers structured AI-guided business innovation through messaging apps to aspiring first-time business creators who have ideas but lack methodology. The product replaces the social validation loop ("friends who say great idea!") with a progress loop — each conversation produces a structured artefact through 22 frameworks across 6 milestones, from raw idea to deployed digital product. Revenue comes from a ~$10/month subscription (M2-M6 access, additional projects) plus a $30-50 one-time custom domain deployment fee. Free-tier projects hosted at `robotville.ai/app/{project}` serve as both user value and organic marketing. No moat today; building toward a compound methodology + structured business data + Brazil WhatsApp/Pix distribution advantage. The core economic bet: unit economics work at $10/month if API costs stay under $5/framework session and enough users complete the journey.

### Key Insights

1. **Customer clarity**: The addressable segment is not "everyone with ideas" — it's the smaller but more motivated subset who've crossed from daydreaming to active intent. Early adopters are those experiencing career disruption or life milestones.
2. **Value proposition**: Differentiation is not "AI for business" (generic) but the specific combination of structured methodology + opinionated pushback + end-to-end pipeline via messaging. The high-concept pitch: "YC mentor in your pocket."
3. **Economic model**: Dual revenue (subscription + deployment fee) with tight but viable unit economics at $10/month. `robotville.ai` hosted projects create a free marketing channel. Biggest risk is API costs at scale.
4. **Defensibility**: Honest — no moat today. Building toward methodology compound, structured data from user completions, and Brazil-specific distribution (WhatsApp + Pix Automatico). The open-source nature of BMAD is both a strength (credibility) and vulnerability (forkable).
