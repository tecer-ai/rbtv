# Brand Process

**Purpose:** Detailed process guide for Milestone 3 (Brand) of the founder module. Creates a preliminary brand identity that translates the validated business concept into a coherent visual and verbal identity, ready to inform prototypation and market validation.

**Goal:** Produce a preliminary brand book that defines who this brand is (archetype, personality), why it exists (purpose, positioning), how it speaks (tone, messaging), and how it looks (visual identity principles) — all grounded in M1 Conception outputs and M2 Validation findings.

## Step 1: Initialize Brand and Extract Inputs

**Inputs:** M1 and M2 applied frameworks, M1 and M2 founder diaries, project memo

**Action:**
1. Create brand folder: `projects/[project]/founder/brand/`
2. Initialize M3 founder diary from the template: [founder_diary.md](../templates/founder_diary.md)
3. Extract brand-relevant inputs from M1/M2:
   - From Working Backwards: customer language, PR headline, leader quote, customer quote
   - From JTBD: emotional and social jobs, how customers want to feel and be perceived
   - From Lean Canvas: UVP statement, customer segments, channels, unfair advantage
   - From Problem-Solution Fit: emotions (before/after), constraints
   - From M2 Validation: validated assumptions about customer pain, market positioning
4. Draft a **Brand Inputs Brief** (1 page max) consolidating these into: target audience, emotional territory, competitive landscape, key messages that resonate
5. Update M3 founder diary Status section to reflect M3 start

**Output:** Brand folder with diary, brand inputs brief

**Framework Reference:** None (structural setup)

---

## Step 2: Identify Brand Archetype

**Inputs:** Brand inputs brief, JTBD emotional/social jobs, Working Backwards customer narrative

**Action:**
1. Read Brand Archetypes framework: [brand_archetypes.md](brand_frameworks/brand_archetypes.md)
2. Follow the framework tasks to:
   - Analyze the 12 universal archetypes through the lens of your customer's emotional and social jobs
   - Select a primary archetype (the dominant brand character)
   - Optionally select a secondary archetype (adds nuance)
   - Define how the archetype manifests in brand voice, visual style, and customer relationship
   - Validate against competitor archetypes to ensure differentiation

**Output:** Brand Archetypes analysis with primary (and optional secondary) archetype

**Framework Reference:** [brand_archetypes.md](brand_frameworks/brand_archetypes.md)

---

## Step 3: Map Brand Identity (Brand Prism)

**Inputs:** Brand Archetypes analysis, JTBD forces, Working Backwards PR, Lean Canvas UVP

**Action:**
1. Read Brand Prism framework: [brand_prism.md](brand_frameworks/brand_prism.md)
2. Follow the framework tasks to:
   - Define all six facets of Kapferer's Brand Prism:
     - **Physique:** Tangible visual/product characteristics
     - **Personality:** Character traits (informed by archetype)
     - **Culture:** Values and beliefs the brand embodies
     - **Relationship:** Type of relationship between brand and customer
     - **Reflection:** How the customer sees themselves through the brand
     - **Self-image:** How the customer feels internally when using the brand
   - Ensure consistency across facets
   - Cross-reference with JTBD emotional/social jobs

**Output:** Brand Prism document with all six facets defined

**Framework Reference:** [brand_prism.md](brand_frameworks/brand_prism.md)

---

## Step 4: Define Purpose (Golden Circle)

**Inputs:** Brand Prism, project memo (Problem, Solution, Tenets), Lean Canvas UVP

**Action:**
1. Read Golden Circle framework: [golden_circle.md](brand_frameworks/golden_circle.md)
2. Follow the framework tasks to:
   - Define **Why:** The purpose, cause, or belief that drives the brand (not "to make money")
   - Define **How:** The specific approach, process, or values that differentiate execution
   - Define **What:** The tangible products or services offered
   - Ensure the Why connects to the customer's emotional job (from JTBD)
   - Validate that the Why is authentic and enduring, not a marketing slogan

**Output:** Golden Circle document with Why, How, What narrative

**Framework Reference:** [golden_circle.md](brand_frameworks/golden_circle.md)

---

## Step 5: Craft Positioning Statement

**Inputs:** Golden Circle, Brand Prism, Lean Canvas (UVP, Customer Segments, Unfair Advantage), M2 TAM/SAM/SOM (competitive context)

**Action:**
1. Read Brand Positioning Statement framework: [brand_positioning.md](brand_frameworks/brand_positioning.md)
2. Follow the framework tasks to:
   - Draft a single-sentence positioning statement following the structure: "For [target segment] who [need/job], [brand] is the [category] that [key benefit], unlike [primary alternative] which [key limitation]."
   - Test against differentiation criteria: is it unique, defensible, relevant, and credible?
   - Create a perceptual map showing competitive position on 2 key dimensions
   - Ensure alignment with Brand Prism, Golden Circle, and Lean Canvas UVP

**Output:** Positioning statement with competitive context and perceptual map

**Framework Reference:** [brand_positioning.md](brand_frameworks/brand_positioning.md)

---

## Step 6: Build Messaging Architecture

**Inputs:** Positioning statement, Brand Prism, Golden Circle, Working Backwards PR, JTBD (customer language)

**Action:**
1. Read Messaging Architecture framework: [messaging_architecture.md](brand_frameworks/messaging_architecture.md)
2. Follow the framework tasks to:
   - Define messaging hierarchy: brand promise → key messages → proof points → calls to action
   - Create audience-specific message variants (early adopters, mainstream, partners, investors)
   - Map messages to customer journey stages (awareness, consideration, decision, retention)
   - Use customer language from JTBD interviews and Working Backwards PR
   - Validate that every message traces back to a validated assumption or framework output

**Output:** Messaging architecture with hierarchical structure and audience variants

**Framework Reference:** [messaging_architecture.md](brand_frameworks/messaging_architecture.md)

---

## Step 7: Define Tone of Voice

**Inputs:** Brand Archetypes, Brand Prism (personality), Messaging Architecture, JTBD (emotional context)

**Action:**
1. Read Tone of Voice Guidelines framework: [tone_of_voice.md](brand_frameworks/tone_of_voice.md)
2. Follow the framework tasks to:
   - Define 3-5 tone dimensions with slider positions (e.g., formal↔casual, serious↔playful)
   - Provide do/don't examples for each dimension
   - Create context-specific tone adjustments (marketing, support, error messages, onboarding)
   - Write sample copy for 3-5 common scenarios using the defined tone
   - Ensure tone is consistent with archetype and brand personality

**Output:** Tone of voice guide with dimensions, examples, and sample copy

**Framework Reference:** [tone_of_voice.md](brand_frameworks/tone_of_voice.md)

---

## Step 8: Synthesize into Project Memo

**Inputs:** All M3 framework documents from Steps 2-7

**Action:**
1. Update project memo Progress > Brand subsection with:
   - Narrative summary of brand identity created
   - Completed frameworks with key insights and links
   - Key brand decisions
2. Refine Tenets section with brand-informed principles
3. Update Next Steps with M4 prototypation readiness

**Output:** Updated project memo with Brand progress

**Framework Reference:** Project Memo template ([project_memo.md](../templates/project_memo.md))

---

## Step 9: Document Key Brand Decisions

**Inputs:** Insights from all M3 steps

**Action:**
1. Review all M3 framework documents for key brand decisions
2. Document archetype selection rationale, positioning trade-offs, tone choices
3. Note any brand decisions that affect M4 prototypation or M5 market validation
4. Document in M3 founder diary with appropriate Type (Decision, Learning)

**Output:** Updated M3 founder diary

**Framework Reference:** Founder Diary template ([founder_diary.md](../templates/founder_diary.md))

---

## Success Criteria

Brand milestone is complete when:

- [ ] Brand Archetypes analysis exists with primary archetype selected and justified
- [ ] Brand Prism document exists with all six facets defined and consistent
- [ ] Golden Circle document exists with authentic Why, How, What
- [ ] Positioning statement exists as a single sentence with competitive differentiation
- [ ] Messaging Architecture exists with hierarchical structure and audience variants
- [ ] Tone of Voice guide exists with dimensions, do/don't examples, and sample copy
- [ ] Project memo Progress > Brand section is populated with key brand decisions
- [ ] Project memo Tenets section reflects brand-informed principles
- [ ] M3 founder diary exists with at least 2 entries documenting key brand decisions
- [ ] Brand outputs are consistent with M1 customer understanding and M2 validation findings
- [ ] Founder can articulate: Who is our brand? What do we stand for? How do we sound? How are we different?
