# AI Writing Anti-Patterns

**The Timid Scribe Problem:** AI smooths edges, generalizes specifics, and flattens distinctive voice into generic uniformity. It accommodates rather than challenges. It resolves tension rather than sustaining it. The result is prose that sounds "good" but says nothing only the writer could say.

---

## Detection Categories

### 1. Over-Symmetry

AI defaults to balanced parallel structures. Real writing is asymmetric.

**Detection:** Count correlative constructions — "Not just X, but Y", "On one hand... on the other", "It's not about X, it's about Y." More than 2 per section = flag.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "It's not just about efficiency — it's about trust." | "Efficiency gets the headlines. Trust is what keeps the system from collapsing at 2 a.m." |
| "On one hand, AI accelerates writing. On the other, it can flatten voice." | "AI makes you faster. Speed is not the problem. The problem is that you stop noticing what you lost." |

### 2. Generic Phrasing

AI defaults to the most common way of saying things. The test: would this sentence appear in 1,000 different essays?

**Detection:** Flag phrases that carry no specificity to THIS essay's argument. Common offenders:
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "This raises important questions about"
- "At its core"
- "The key takeaway is"
- "Moving forward"

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape, writing is changing." | "Six months ago I typed every word. Now I argue with a machine about comma placement." |
| "This raises important questions about authenticity." | "If the reader can't tell, does it matter? I think it does, but not for the reasons people assume." |

### 3. Edge Erosion

AI softens strong positions. "Perhaps" where "clearly" was meant. "May" where "does" was meant. The conviction level drops between the user's raw thinking and the draft.

**Detection:** Compare draft claims against the Essay Seed. Has the draft hedged what the user stated directly? Look for: "perhaps", "arguably", "one might say", "it could be argued that", "to some extent."

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "This approach may not be the most effective way to address the issue." | "This approach fails." |
| "One could argue that the current system is perhaps somewhat outdated." | "The current system is outdated. Everyone knows it. Nobody says it." |

### 4. Premature Resolution

AI wraps up neatly. Real essays leave productive tension. The reader should walk away unsettled — not reassured.

**Detection:** Does the conclusion resolve all tensions the body raised? Does it end with a tidy lesson? Does it feel like the essay is patting the reader on the head?

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "In the end, the key is finding the right balance between AI assistance and human creativity." | "I don't know where the line is. I'm not sure there is one. What I know is that the relief I felt when the words came easy should have been a warning." |

### 5. Emotional Flattening

AI writes ABOUT emotions without embodying them. It tells when it must show.

**Detection:** Are emotional moments narrated from outside ("she felt relieved") or experienced from inside ("the relief was physical — the kind that lives in your shoulders")? Is the emotional register consistent with the voice profile?

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "She felt a sense of relief and accomplishment after finishing the project." | "The relief hit her shoulders first — a loosening she didn't know she'd been holding for weeks." |
| "The experience was both challenging and rewarding." | "I hated every minute of it. I'd do it again tomorrow." |

### 6. False Sophistication

AI adds complexity that sounds smart but says nothing. Nested qualifications, hedged hedges, subordinate clauses stacked three deep.

**Detection:** Can the sentence be simplified without losing meaning? If yes, the complexity was decorative. Look for: sentences over 40 words with multiple qualifications, triple-nested "while... although... despite" constructions.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "While it is certainly true that, notwithstanding the various challenges that have been documented in recent literature, the fundamental paradigm underlying the approach remains, at least in principle, broadly applicable..." | "The approach works. The literature says otherwise. Here's why the literature is looking at the wrong variable." |

### 7. List-ification

AI loves lists and bullet points even when prose serves better. Lists signal "I organized the information" but break narrative flow.

**Detection:** Would this section read better as flowing paragraphs? Are the bullet points genuinely parallel items, or are they sequential thoughts forced into list format?

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "The benefits include: (1) faster iteration, (2) broader perspective, (3) reduced cognitive load." | "You iterate faster because the machine handles the scaffolding. You see more because it brings references you forgot. And the weird part — you think less about mechanics, so you think more about meaning." |

### 8. Recap Syndrome

AI summarizes what it just said. The ending restates rather than extends.

**Detection:** Does the final section begin with "In summary," "As we've seen," "To recap," "The key takeaway is"? Does the conclusion add new insight or just compress the body?

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "In summary, AI writing tools offer both opportunities and challenges. As we've seen, the key is maintaining authenticity while leveraging efficiency." | "I started this essay thinking I was writing about AI. I'm ending it thinking about what I was afraid to say without it." |

---

## Usage in Workflow

**Step 08 (Drafting):** After each section, check against these anti-patterns. Focus on: generic phrasing, edge erosion, emotional flattening.

**Step 10 (Critical Review):** The AI-Pattern Audit critic loads this file and scans the complete essay against all 8 categories. Each finding includes the offending passage and a human-sounding alternative.

**Step 11 (Synthesis):** During the "What do you notice?" ritual, newly discovered anti-patterns specific to this writer are added to the persistent style guide's Anti-Patterns section.
