## Problem Solved

How do you achieve professional-quality image generation when generic descriptions produce amateurish results?

---

## Technique Overview

Use precise photography, cinematography, and art terminology (lens characteristics, lighting setups, camera settings, quality parameters) to activate professional-quality image generation capabilities and produce commercial-grade output.

---

## When to Apply

| Ideal For | Avoid For |
|-----------|-----------|
| Product photography, commercial portraits, marketing visuals requiring professional quality | Quick sketches, experimental generations, or artistic exploration where technical precision isn't needed |
| Photorealistic output where camera/lens characteristics matter | Abstract art, illustrations, or stylized work where technical specs are irrelevant |
| Scenarios where first-generation quality must match professional standards | Iterative workflows where quality emerges through conversation rather than initial specification |
| Commercial work requiring specific technical quality (resolution, sharpness, depth of field) | Simple, single-subject images where full technical specification is overkill |

---

## Application Pattern

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | **Specify Lens Characteristics** | Include focal length and aperture: "85mm f/1.4", "24-70mm f/2.8", "macro lens". Focal length affects perspective; aperture affects depth of field |
| 2 | **Define Lighting Setup** | Specify lighting type: "softbox", "golden hour", "Rembrandt lighting", "three-point lighting". Include direction and quality (soft, harsh, dramatic) |
| 3 | **Set Camera Parameters** | Include relevant settings: "shallow depth of field", "wide angle", "telephoto compression", "tilt-shift effect" if compositionally relevant |
| 4 | **Specify Quality Parameters** | Include resolution and detail: "8K ultra-high definition", "crystalline detail", "commercial quality", "print-ready" |
| 5 | **Add Material/Texture Specs** | For product photography: specify surface materials, reflections, textures: "polished black marble", "matte finish", "glossy surface" |
| 6 | **Include Composition Terms** | Technical composition: "rule of thirds", "leading lines", "bokeh background", "selective focus" |
| 7 | **Verify Technical Consistency** | Ensure technical specs work together: e.g., "85mm f/1.4" with "shallow depth of field" is consistent |

**Key Considerations:**
- **Precision over verbosity:** Use precise technical terms rather than long descriptions
- **Professional terminology:** Use established photography/cinematography vocabulary
- **Quality activation:** Technical terms activate professional-quality generation capabilities

---

## Variations

| Variation | When to Use | Difference from Core |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Photography-Focused** | Product shots, portraits, commercial photography | Emphasizes lens specs, studio lighting, camera settings |
| **Cinematography-Focused** | Cinematic scenes, dramatic compositions | Emphasizes camera movement, shot types, lighting for mood |
| **Quality-Focused** | High-resolution, detail-critical outputs | Emphasizes resolution specs, sharpness, detail parameters |
| **Minimal Technical** | Quick professional results | Key technical terms only: lens + lighting + quality |

---

## Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why It Fails | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Generic quality terms** | "Good quality" or "professional" provides no actionable direction | Use specific technical terms: "8K", "85mm f/1.4", "softbox lighting" |
| **Technical contradictions** | Conflicting specs ("wide angle" with "telephoto compression") confuse the model | Ensure technical parameters are consistent and complementary |
| **Over-technical specification** | Too many technical terms without visual context dilutes important elements | Balance technical specs with visual description; 3-5 key technical terms usually sufficient |
| **Missing quality activation** | Without technical vocabulary, models default to generic quality | Always include at least one quality parameter: resolution, lens spec, or lighting setup |

## Quality Checklist

Before generating images with technical vocabulary, verify:

- [ ] **Lens characteristics specified:** Focal length and aperture included if relevant (e.g., "85mm f/1.4")
- [ ] **Lighting setup defined:** Type, direction, quality explicitly stated (e.g., "softbox", "golden hour")
- [ ] **Quality parameters included:** Resolution or quality spec mentioned (e.g., "8K", "commercial quality")
- [ ] **Technical consistency verified:** All technical specs work together (no contradictions)
- [ ] **Precision over verbosity:** Using precise technical terms rather than long descriptions
- [ ] **Professional terminology:** Using established photography/cinematography vocabulary
- [ ] **Balance maintained:** Technical specs balanced with visual description (not over-technical)
