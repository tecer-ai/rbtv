# Competitive Landscape Framework

## Overview

Understanding competitors is not about copying features — it's about understanding **how the market currently addresses the customer's job**. This includes direct competitors, indirect alternatives, geographic benchmarks (how mature markets solve this), and cross-industry analogues (how similar problems are solved elsewhere).

**Core concept:** Before you can articulate why your solution fits better, you must understand what alternatives exist, what works, what doesn't, and where the gaps are.

---

## Analysis Dimensions

### 1. Current Alternatives

| Category | What to Capture |
|----------|-----------------|
| Direct Competitors | Products/services solving the same problem for the same customer |
| Indirect Alternatives | Other categories addressing the same job (spreadsheets, manual processes, consultants) |
| Non-Consumption | Why some customers do nothing (awareness, cost, complexity, inertia) |

### 2. Geographic Benchmarking

| Dimension | US Market | China Market |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Leading Players | Top 3-5 companies, market share | Top companies (BAT ecosystem, super-apps) |
| Solution Maturity | Early/Growth/Mature stage | Stage, unique trajectory vs. Western |
| Business Models | Subscription, transaction, freemium | Often different patterns |
| Regulatory Environment | Key regulations shaping market | Regulations and impact |
| Lessons for Our Context | What can be imported, adapted, or avoided | Unique approaches to consider |

### 3. Cross-Industry Analogues

Abstract the core problem pattern to find solutions in adjacent industries:

| Example Problem Pattern | Potential Analogues |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Asset liquidity | Auto finance, invoice factoring, art-backed lending |
| Matching/marketplace | Dating, job boards, real estate |
| Compliance/verification | KYC/AML, identity verification, background checks |
| Subscription management | SaaS, media streaming, fitness |

### 4. Competitor Analysis

For each major competitor:

| Dimension | What to Capture |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Strengths | Product excellence, distribution, brand, team |
| Weaknesses | Product gaps, service issues, limitations |
| Target Customer | Segment, size, geography optimized for |
| Pricing Model | Subscription tiers, usage-based, freemium |
| Go-to-Market | Sales, PLG, partnerships |
| Technology | Tech stack, integrations, platform dependencies |
| Funding/Stage | Investment raised, growth trajectory |

### 5. Competitive Positioning

Choose 2 dimensions that matter most to customers:
- Price vs. Feature Depth
- Specialization vs. Breadth
- Self-service vs. High-touch
- SMB vs. Enterprise
- Speed vs. Accuracy
- Simplicity vs. Power

Identify "white space" (unoccupied quadrants) for differentiation.

## Output Structure

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| 1. Current Alternatives | Direct/indirect competitors, non-consumption analysis |
| 2. Geographic Benchmarking | US/China market analysis with lessons |
| 3. Cross-Industry Analogues | Problem pattern, analogues, transferable insights |
| 4. Competitor Deep-Dive | Strengths/weaknesses per competitor |
| 5. Competitive Positioning | 2x2 map, feature comparison, positioning analysis |
| 6. Threats & Opportunities | Threat inventory, opportunity inventory, prioritization |
| 7. Assumptions for Validation | Full inventory with categories and validation methods |

## Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Relying on training data | MUST use web research for all claims |
| Ignoring non-traditional competitors | Include indirect alternatives and workarounds |
| Ignoring geographic context | Benchmark US and China markets |
| Surface-level analysis | Analyze strengths, weaknesses, positioning |
| Unverified differentiation claims | Tag claims as assumptions |
| Analysis without action | Connect findings to opportunities and M2 validation |
