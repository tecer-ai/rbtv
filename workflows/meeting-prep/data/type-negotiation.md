# Meeting Type: Negotiation

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What is being negotiated? Contract, pricing, terms, scope, renewal? | Shapes the entire preparation structure |
| Who will be on the other side? Roles and decision authority? | Who can say yes, who can block |
| What stage is the negotiation — opening, mid, final? | How aggressive or conciliatory to be |

### Relationship History
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What's the history between you and the other party? | Leverage, trust level, prior commitments |
| Any prior agreements, contracts, or deals between you? | Precedents they'll reference |
| What's the current relationship temperature? | Whether to prioritize preservation or extraction |

### Objectives
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What's your ideal outcome? | Best-case target |
| What's the minimum you'd accept? | Walk-away line |
| What are you willing to concede? | Concession planning |
| What's your BATNA (best alternative if this fails)? | Your actual leverage |

### Their Position
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What do you think they want most? | Their likely opening and priorities |
| What's their BATNA — what happens if they walk away? | Their leverage and pressure |
| What constraints are they under — budget, timeline, internal politics? | Pressure points and empathy angles |
| What concessions might they offer? | Negotiation space |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Do you want to anchor first or let them open? | Opening tactic |
| Are there non-monetary items you can trade? | Creative deal-making options |
| What topics are off-limits or explosive? | Avoidance zones |
| What impression do you want to leave regardless of outcome? | Long-term relationship calibration |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format? | Pacing |
| Who's on your side? Roles? | Coordination — good cop/bad cop, technical backup |

## Output Skeleton

### {Title} — Negotiation Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Contexto
Table: negotiation subject, stage, parties, format, duration, relationship temperature.

#### Postura geral
Overall negotiation approach — collaborative vs. competitive, tone, what to project.

#### Relationship History
Key facts about the history between parties. Prior agreements, trust level, any leverage from past interactions.

#### Position Map
Table with two columns — Your Position vs. Their Likely Position: ideal outcome, minimum acceptable, known constraints, BATNA.

#### Concession Ladder
Ordered list of what you can give up, from least to most costly. Each with: what you concede, what you ask for in return, when to deploy it.

#### Red Lines
Numbered list of non-negotiable points. For each: the line, why it's non-negotiable, what to say if they push on it.

#### Opening Strategy
How to open the negotiation — who speaks first, anchoring tactic, first 5 minutes script.

#### Key Phrases
Table: situation → what to say. Prepared language for predictable moments — their objections, your concessions, deadlock-breaking.

#### Things NOT to Say
Table: statement to avoid → why. Language that would weaken your position or damage the relationship.

#### Deadlock Playbook
If negotiation stalls: 3 options to break deadlock, ordered by escalation (reframe → introduce new variable → adjourn with specific next step).

#### Walk-Away Protocol
When to walk away, how to do it gracefully, what to say, how to leave the door open.
