# Meeting Type: Partnership / Alliance

---

## Discovery Questions

### Context
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What kind of partnership — integration, co-sell, referral, joint venture? | Shapes the value proposition |
| Who is the other company? What do they do? | Basic framing |
| Who will be in the room? Roles? | Decision authority and technical depth needed |
| How did this come about? | Temperature and existing expectations |

### Relationship History
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Any prior interactions with this company? | Trust level, prior context |
| Do you share any customers, investors, or partners? | Connection points and potential conflicts |
| Is there any competitive tension between you? | Areas to handle carefully |

### Mutual Value
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What do you bring that they need? | Your side of the value equation |
| What do they bring that you need? | Their side of the value equation |
| What would a successful partnership look like in 6 months? | Concrete vision to work toward |
| What could go wrong? | Risk awareness |

### Objectives
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What's the minimum viable partnership — smallest useful first step? | Pragmatic entry point |
| What's the dream outcome? | Ambition alignment |
| What would make you walk away? | Deal-breakers |
| What impression do you want to leave? | Tone calibration |

### Strategy
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Do you want to propose something specific or explore openly? | Meeting structure |
| What questions do you want to ask them? | Discovery priorities |
| Any sensitive topics — pricing, exclusivity, IP? | Avoidance or careful handling zones |

### Logistics
| Question | What this reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| Date, time, duration, format? | Pacing |
| Who's on your side? | Coordination |

## Output Skeleton

### {Title} — Partnership Cheat Sheet — {Date}

#### Contexto
Table: partnership type, parties, participants, format, duration, relationship temperature.

#### Postura geral
Overall approach — exploration vs. proposal, tone, what to project. Balance between enthusiasm and pragmatism.

#### Relationship Context
What's known about the relationship between the two organizations. Shared connections, prior interactions, competitive tensions.

#### Value Map
Two-column table: What We Bring → What They Bring. Concrete capabilities, assets, access, or resources each side contributes.

#### Alignment Points
Numbered list of areas where interests clearly align. Each with a brief note on why this matters to both sides.

#### Deal-Breaker Signals
What to watch for that indicates this partnership won't work. Red flags in their language, priorities, or constraints.

#### Discovery Questions
Questions to ask during the meeting to validate alignment. Each with "what this reveals" annotation.

#### Proposal Framework
If proposing: the minimum viable partnership — what it looks like, what each side commits to, timeline, success criteria. If exploring: the questions that would lead to a joint proposal.

#### Things to Say
Table: situation → what to say. Language for key moments — describing your value, asking about their needs, proposing next steps.

#### Things NOT to Say
Table: statement to avoid → why. Language that would create wrong expectations or reveal too much too early.

#### Next Steps (graduated)
Table by alignment level (strong/moderate/weak): what to propose as the concrete next step.
