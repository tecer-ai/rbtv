# Problem Trees

Problem trees (also called issue trees, logic trees, or decision trees) are visual diagrams that decompose complex problems into hierarchical sub-questions.

---

## Core Concept

A problem tree flows from a single root question on the left to smaller, more specific questions on the right. Each branch level must be MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive).

---

## Types of Problem Trees

| Type | Root Question | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|---------|
| **Diagnostic** | "Why is this happening?" | Identify root causes | "Why are profits declining?" |
| **Solution-focused** | "How can we solve this?" | Explore interventions | "How can we increase market share?" |
| **Decision** | "Should we do X?" | Evaluate options | "Should we enter new market?" |

---

## Structure

| Layer | Contains | Question Type |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| 1st (Root) | Main question/problem | Yes/No hypothesis |
| 2nd | Major categories | Yes/No hypothesis |
| 3rd | Sub-questions | Open-ended exploration |
| 4th | Specific data needs | Open-ended, detailed |

**Recommended:** 3-4 layers, 2-5 branches per node

---

## Building Process

| Step | Action | Output |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Define the core problem | Single root question |
| 2 | Identify major categories | 2-5 MECE branches |
| 3 | Break each category into sub-questions | Layer 3 questions |
| 4 | Add data needs where required | Layer 4 details |
| 5 | Verify MECE at each level | Adjusted tree |
| 6 | Review logical flow | Final tree |

---

## Question Types

| Type | When to Use | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Hypothesis (Yes/No)** | Layers 1-2 | "Can we grow profits to $100M?" |
| **Open-ended** | Layers 3-4 | "What types of products does client sell?" |

Start with hypothesis questions to focus the tree; use open-ended questions deeper to encourage exploration.

---

## Example: Profit Decline

```
Why is profit declining?
│
├── Is revenue declining?
│   ├── Is price declining?
│   │   ├── What are competitor prices?
│   │   └── What is our pricing strategy?
│   └── Is volume declining?
│       ├── What is market growth rate?
│       └── Are we losing market share?
│
└── Are costs increasing?
    ├── Are fixed costs increasing?
    │   ├── What are facility costs?
    │   └── What are overhead changes?
    └── Are variable costs increasing?
        ├── What are raw material costs?
        └── What are labor costs?
```

---

## Example: Market Entry Decision

```
Should we enter the Bulgarian market?
│
├── Is it financially attractive?
│   ├── What is market size/growth?
│   ├── What margins are achievable?
│   └── What is required investment?
│
├── Can we compete effectively?
│   ├── What capabilities do we have?
│   ├── Who are the competitors?
│   └── What is our differentiation?
│
└── What are the risks?
    ├── What are regulatory barriers?
    ├── What are operational risks?
    └── What are reputational risks?
```

## Best Practices

| Practice | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Orient paper horizontally** | More room for branching |
| **Think before writing** | 30-60 seconds planning prevents rework |
| **Use frameworks as starting points** | Revenue/Cost, 3Cs, 4Ps are MECE by design |
| **Add "color commentary"** | Industry-specific insights show depth |
| **Test MECE after each layer** | Catch gaps and overlaps early |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Non-MECE branches | Overlaps cause double-counting; gaps miss issues | Apply MECE test at each level |
| Too shallow (2 layers) | Doesn't reach actionable insights | Add layers until data needs emerge |
| Too deep (5+ layers) | Gets lost in weeds | Stop at 3-4 layers |
| Jumping around the tree | Confuses audience | Work systematically left-to-right, top-to-bottom |
| No hypothesis questions | Tree becomes aimless data gathering | Frame top layers as Yes/No |

---

## Using Problem Trees

| Phase | How to Use |
|-------|------------|
| **Problem definition** | Structure your understanding of the issue |
| **Analysis** | Guide data collection systematically |
| **Communication** | Walk stakeholders through your logic |
| **Recommendation** | Ensure all bases covered before concluding |
