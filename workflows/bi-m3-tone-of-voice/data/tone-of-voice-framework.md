# Tone of Voice Framework

**Purpose:** Define how a brand sounds — not what it says (that's Messaging Architecture), but how it says it. Using tone dimensions, do/don't examples, and context adjustments.

---

## Tone Dimensions Menu

Select 3-5 dimensions and position your brand on each slider (1-5 scale):

| Dimension | Pole 1 (Score 1) | Pole 2 (Score 5) | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|-------|
| **Formal ↔ Casual** | Structured, conventional | Relaxed, conversational | Affects word choice, contractions |
| **Serious ↔ Playful** | Earnest, focused | Light, humorous | Affects use of levity |
| **Humble ↔ Confident** | Modest, understated | Assertive, bold | Affects claim strength |
| **Technical ↔ Accessible** | Expert vocabulary | Plain language | Affects assumed knowledge |
| **Reserved ↔ Enthusiastic** | Calm, measured | Energetic, expressive | Affects emotional display |
| **Respectful ↔ Irreverent** | Conventional, polite | Challenging, unconventional | Affects authority stance |
| **Abstract ↔ Concrete** | Conceptual, philosophical | Specific, tangible | Affects example usage |

### Scoring Guidance

- **1** = Extreme left pole
- **2** = Lean left
- **3** = Neutral (avoid defaulting here — neutrality = no personality)
- **4** = Lean right
- **5** = Extreme right pole

## Archetype-to-Tone Tendencies

Use as starting intuitions, not rigid rules:

| Archetype | Natural Tone Tendencies |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Hero/Explorer** | Confident, direct, action-oriented |
| **Sage/Creator** | Thoughtful, precise, inventive |

| **Ruler** | Authoritative, formal, commanding |

| Scenario | Context | Reader State |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| **Welcome Email** | First communication after signup | Hopeful, uncertain |
| **Error Message** | Something went wrong | Frustrated, anxious |
| **Feature Announcement** | New capability shipped | Curious, busy |
| **Support Response** | Customer asked for help | Seeking resolution |

## Communication Contexts for Adjustments

Tone adjusts by context; personality stays constant:

| Context | Reader State | Typical Adjustment |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Marketing** (website, ads, social) | Distracted, skeptical | Dial UP aspiration and personality |
| **Onboarding** (welcome flows, setup) | Hopeful, uncertain | Dial UP encouragement, dial DOWN complexity |
| **Error/Support** (errors, help) | Frustrated, anxious | Dial UP empathy and directness, dial DOWN playfulness |
| **Documentation** (help docs, guides) | Task-focused, impatient | Dial UP precision, dial DOWN personality |
| **Social Media** (posts, replies) | Casual, open | Dial UP personality, allow more informality |

### Adjustment Rules

- Maximum adjustment: +/- 1 from baseline
- Personality modulates in intensity, does not transform
- 1-2 dimensions should NEVER shift (non-negotiable core)

## Pitfalls

### 1. Vague Descriptions
"Be friendly," "be professional," "be authentic" — every brand claims these. They give writers no calibration tool.

**Instead:** Use slider scales with specific positions.

### 2. No Context Adjustments
Same playful tone for marketing and error messages damages trust.

**Instead:** Define explicit adjustments per context.

### 3. Generic Examples
"Hello, valued customer!" teaches nothing brand-specific.

**Instead:** Use actual brand product, terminology, and messaging.

### 4. Tone Without Personality First
Tone is the audible expression of Brand Archetypes and Prism Personality. Without those, tone choices are arbitrary.

**Instead:** Complete Brand Archetypes and Brand Prism before Tone of Voice.

### 5. Guide as Constraint
If nobody uses the guide because it feels restrictive, the framing is wrong.

**Instead:** Position as decision-making tool — "here is how we sound" not "here is what you must not do."

## Prerequisites

Before starting Tone of Voice:

- Brand Archetypes complete (provides character foundation)
- Brand Prism complete (provides personality facet)
- Golden Circle complete (provides emotional register from Why)
- Messaging Architecture complete (provides content to apply tone to) — RECOMMENDED
- Brand Positioning complete (provides register calibration) — RECOMMENDED

This is the **final framework in M3 Brand**. Completing it marks M3 as complete.
