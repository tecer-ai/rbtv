# Working Backwards Framework Knowledge

**Purpose:** Reference knowledge for the Working Backwards (PR/FAQ) framework methodology.

---

## Framework Overview

Working Backwards starts from a **future press release and FAQs** describing a product at launch, in customer language.

### The PR/FAQ Structure

**Press Release (1 page):**
1. Who is the customer?
2. What problem are you solving?
3. What is the solution (in customer language)?
4. Why will customers adopt it?

**FAQ (split into two parts):**
- **External FAQ:** Questions customers, partners, and stakeholders would ask
- **Internal FAQ:** Feasibility, economics, dependencies, strategy — culminating in "Is it worth doing?"

---

## Task Sequence

| Task | Goal | Key Output |
|------|------|------------|
| 1 | Clarify Customer And Problem | Customer & Problem Brief |
| 2 | Draft The Press Release | Press Release v1 |
| 3 | Draft The External FAQ | External FAQ v1 |
| 4 | Draft The Internal FAQ | Internal FAQ v1 with "Is it worth doing?" |

---

## Press Release Structure

1. **Headline** — Core customer benefit (not technology)
2. **Subheading** — Names primary customer and what changes
3. **Dateline** — Location and future launch date
4. **Intro Paragraph** — Context, category
5. **Problem Paragraph** — Vivid description of customer pain
6. **Solution Paragraph** — How product solves pain
7. **Leader Quote** — Why this matters
8. **How It Works** — Typical customer journey
9. **Customer Quote** — Hypothetical from archetypal customer
10. **Getting Started** — Access, pricing, onboarding

---

## External FAQ Themes

- Product understanding ("What is it?", "How does it work?")
- Eligibility and fit ("Is this for companies like mine?")
- Pricing and commercial terms
- Integration and migration
- Data, privacy, and trust
- Support and reliability

---

## Internal FAQ Themes

- **Feasibility and resources** (team, technology, time)
- **Economics** (TAM/SAM/SOM, unit economics, pricing logic)
- **Dependencies and risks** (partners, regulation, data, platform risk)
- **Strategic fit** (alignment with vision, differentiation)
- **Success metrics and kill criteria**

---

## Key Questions to Answer

**For Customer & Problem Brief:**
- Who is the primary customer (job title, company size, industry)?
- What job are they trying to get done?
- What are their current workarounds?
- What are the painful frictions?

**For Internal FAQ:**
- What capabilities must we build that we don't have?
- What are the top technical unknowns?
- What has to be true about customer behavior for unit economics to work?
- What would make us kill this project after 3-6 months?

---

## Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Writing for stakeholders, not customers** — Strip internal language
2. **Treating PR/FAQ as marketing copy** — Write awkward questions into Internal FAQ
3. **Skipping the Internal FAQ** — Never consider PR "done" without "Is it worth doing?"
4. **Over-specifying implementation** — Lock what must be true for customer, not how to implement
5. **Never killing ideas** — Define kill criteria in advance; record kills as method successes

---

## Validation Checklist

**Customer & Problem Brief:**
- [ ] Can name at least 3 real organizations/people who fit
- [ ] Problem statement makes sense without mentioning product
- [ ] Third party can paraphrase after one read
- [ ] At least 2 current alternatives listed

**Press Release:**
- [ ] Fits on one page
- [ ] Customer and problem clear in first two paragraphs
- [ ] Outside person finds it compelling
- [ ] No internal jargon

**External FAQ:**
- [ ] At least 10 substantial questions with concrete answers
- [ ] Each PR promise has FAQ clarifying limits
- [ ] Assumptions tagged for validation

**Internal FAQ:**
- [ ] Written answer to "Is it worth doing?"
- [ ] Top risks and assumptions listed
- [ ] No hidden dependencies
