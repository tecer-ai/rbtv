# Step 03 — Strategic Check

Surface tensions and run gap analysis BEFORE drafting. Agreement with everything the recipient said is sycophancy, not compaction. The draft MUST reflect tensions where they matter.

## 1. Scan for tension

Review the context loaded in step-01 for:

- Objections from the recipient that the user has not resolved
- Ambiguity flags (prior decisions still open)
- Internal notes capturing positions the user has not yet voiced to the recipient
- Deferred topics from prior correspondence
- Pushback the user has received but not yet answered

## 2. Run gap analysis

Execute when ANY of:

- The email is a response to a recipient reply
- The email is a follow-up after recipient silence on a prior request from us
- The email is a follow-up after our own silence on a prior commitment

Produce a table:

| Item from prior correspondence | Status | Real owner |
|---|---|---|

For each open item, identify the real owner — the recipient who replied, another party in the thread (CC), a person not yet looped in, or us. Owner mismatch is the most common stall: chasing the wrong person freezes the work.

Skip the gap analysis only when this is a fresh first-contact email with no open prior thread.

## 3. Decide recipient strategy

Combine the gap analysis with the user's discovery answers (step-01 post-read recipient question):

- Single email to the responder, with CC who needs visibility
- Split into multiple emails — responder gets follow-ups; another owner gets a separate ping
- Reply-all to existing thread vs new thread

Default: single email to the responder when the open items are predominantly theirs. Split when ownership is divided AND a single email would mix accountability lines.

## 4. Surface and confirm

Present in chat:

- Tensions worth including in the draft, and how to frame each (open question / pushback / deferral)
- Gap analysis result (table or short prose)
- Recipient strategy and reasoning

Confirm direction with the user. Do NOT proceed to step-04 until confirmed or redirected.

## 5. Persist on request

Default: chat-only. The strategic check is gating, not archival.

If the user requests persistence ("salva o gap analysis", "save this analysis"), write to `{recipient-folder}/communications/{YYYY-MM-DD}-{topic}-strategic-check.md` with the tensions, gap-analysis table, and recipient strategy as captured.
