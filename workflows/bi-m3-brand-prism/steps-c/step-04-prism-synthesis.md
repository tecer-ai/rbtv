## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Define Relationship Facet

Start from archetype relationship type:

> "Your Brand Archetypes defined a relationship type: [relationship type from archetypes]
>
> The Relationship facet expands this into a complete brand-customer dynamic."

**Elicit relationship dimensions:**

**A. Brand Promises** — Ask:
> "What does the customer GET from this relationship? Complete: 'We will always...' or 'We promise to...'"

Examples:
- "We will always explain why, not just what."
- "We promise to challenge you to improve, not just make you comfortable."

**B. Customer Expectations** — Ask:
> "What does the customer need to BRING? Complete: 'We expect that you...' or 'This works best when you...'"

Examples:
- "We expect that you invest time to learn."
- "This works best when you share honest feedback."

**C. Situational Behaviors** — For each moment:

1. **Onboarding** — Ask:
   > "How does your brand welcome a new customer? What's the relationship stance?"

2. **Success** — Ask:
   > "How does your brand celebrate when a customer achieves something?"

3. **Failure/Frustration** — Ask:
   > "When something goes wrong, how does the relationship respond?"

4. **Departure** — Ask:
   > "If a customer decides to leave, how does the relationship handle it?"

**D. Relationship Statement** — Draft together:
> "Our relationship with customers is like [analogy]. We [promise]. In return, we ask that they [expectation]. When things go wrong, we [crisis behavior]."

**Ground in JTBD forces:**
> "Does this relationship address the anxieties from your JTBD? Does it respect customer habits?"

**Output:**
- Named relationship type
- 2-3 brand promises
- 2-3 customer expectations
- 4 situational behaviors
- 1-paragraph Relationship Statement

### 2. Consistency Test

Present all 6 facets together in prism layout:

> "Now we test the complete prism for internal consistency."

**Test each facet pair:**

1. **Physique ↔ Personality**
   > "Does a [Personality] brand naturally create a [Physique] experience?"

2. **Personality ↔ Culture**
   > "Does a brand that acts like [Personality] naturally believe [Culture]?"

3. **Culture ↔ Relationship**
   > "Does a brand that believes [Culture] naturally relate to customers as [Relationship]?"

4. **Relationship ↔ Reflection**
   > "Does the [Relationship] dynamic produce customers who appear [Reflection]?"

5. **Reflection ↔ Self-Image**
   > "Can customers appear [Reflection] to others while feeling [Self-Image] internally?"

6. **Physique ↔ Self-Image**
   > "Does the [Physique] experience deliver the [Self-Image] feeling?"

**For each contradiction found:**
- Identify which facet is the outlier
- Propose revision options
- Get founder decision
- Document revision and reasoning

### 3. Competitor Contrast Test

> "If I read your prism narrative, would it be interchangeable with a major competitor's?"

If yes:
- Identify which facets lack distinctiveness
- Sharpen with more specific language
- Document the differentiation choices

### 4. Write Prism Narrative

Compose the complete narrative:

> "Our brand looks like [Physique], acts like [Personality], believes [Culture], relates to customers as [Relationship], helps them appear [Reflection], and makes them feel [Self-Image]."

**Test readability:**
> "Does this read as a single coherent character? Or does it feel like a collage?"

If collage, identify the dissonant element and revise.
