# Messaging Architecture Framework

**Core Principle:** Every word a brand produces must trace upward to a single brand promise and downward to concrete evidence.

---

## Hierarchy Levels

### Level 1: Brand Promise

Single customer-facing statement (max 15 words) that:
- Combines emotional aspiration (from Golden Circle Why) with rational mechanism (from Positioning benefit)
- Is customer-facing — a real customer would nod and say "yes, that's what I want"
- Is emotionally resonant — connects to a feeling (confidence, relief, pride), not a feature
- Is differentiated — a competitor cannot claim the identical promise

**Structure patterns:**
- "[Emotional aspiration] through [rational mechanism]"
- "[What changes for you] because [why we exist]"

### Level 2: Key Messages

3-5 messages per audience that:
- Support the brand promise (every message traces upward)
- Address what that specific audience cares about
- Use language the audience actually uses (from JTBD interviews)
- Are outcomes/beliefs/emotional shifts — NOT features

**Traceability format:** `[Source: JTBD primary job]` or `[Source: M2 assumption #3]`

### Level 3: Proof Points

2-3 evidence items per message. Types:
- **Data:** M2 validation results, survey percentages, market sizing
- **Customer quote:** Verbatim from JTBD interviews (not paraphrased)
- **Feature-benefit:** Capability framed as outcome ("reduces 4 hours to 20 minutes")
- **Third-party:** Analyst citations, press, advisor endorsements

### Level 4: Calls to Action

CTAs mapped to journey stages and channels:
- Awareness → "Learn more" (read, watch, download)
- Consideration → "Try or compare" (trial, demo, case studies)
- Decision → "Buy or sign up" (account, plan, contract)
- Retention → "Expand or refer" (upgrade, invite, review)

## Audience Definitions

| Audience | Focus | Primary Source |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Early Adopters | Pain severity, tolerance for rough edges | JTBD primary job, push forces |
| Mainstream Customers | Job completion, anxieties, pull forces | JTBD related jobs, pull forces |
| Partners | Reach extension, integration value | Lean Canvas Channels, Unfair Advantage |
| Investors | Market size, metrics, problem severity | Lean Canvas Problem, M2 TAM/SAM/SOM |

---

## Journey Stage Mindsets

| Stage | Mindset | CTA Type |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Awareness | Curious, skeptical, time-constrained | Learn more |
| Consideration | Analytical, risk-aware, seeking proof | Try or compare |
| Decision | Needs reassurance, minimize switching cost | Buy or sign up |
| Retention | Evaluating ROI, looking for deeper value | Expand or refer |

---

## Quality Filters

**For Messages:**
- Not a feature in disguise
- Has traceability to framework output or validated data
- Doesn't overlap with another message for same audience
- Uses customer language, not internal vocabulary

**For Proof Points:**
- Has documented source
- Has explicit type (data/quote/feature-benefit/third-party)
- Has status (validated/hypothetical)
- At least one per message from JTBD interviews
- At least one per message from M2 validation

---

## Pitfalls

1. **Writing slogans instead of strategy** — Promise is internal direction, not a tagline
2. **Treating all audiences the same** — Distinct message sets per audience required
3. **Proof points without sources** — Unattributed claims are opinions, not evidence
4. **Feature-stuffing messages** — Messages are outcomes, features are proof points
5. **Skipping journey mapping** — CTAs activate the architecture operationally
6. **Ignoring M2 data** — Validation findings are mandatory inputs
