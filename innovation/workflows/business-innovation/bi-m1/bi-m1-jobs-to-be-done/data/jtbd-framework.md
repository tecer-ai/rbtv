# Jobs-to-be-Done Framework Knowledge

**Purpose:** Reference knowledge for the JTBD methodology — understanding what job customers hire products to do.

---

## Framework Overview

Customers do not simply "buy products"; they **hire** them to make progress in specific situations, relative to imperfect alternatives.

### The Core Concept

When a person in a given context wants to achieve an outcome, they consider options (including spreadsheets, email, doing nothing) and "hire" the one that best balances functional, emotional, and social jobs.

### Job Statement Structure

**"When [situation/trigger], I want to [progress], so I can [ultimate outcome]."**

---

## Job Types

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| Functional | What they are doing | "Get accurate data to stakeholders" |
| Emotional | How they want to feel | "Feel in control of my team's output" |
| Social | How they want to be perceived | "Look competent to my leadership" |

---

## The Four Forces

Every hiring decision involves competing forces:

| Force | Direction | Questions |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Push of Present** | Away from status quo | What's painful about the current way? |
| **Pull of New** | Toward new solution | What's attractive about alternatives? |
| **Anxieties** | Resisting change | What fears exist about adopting something new? |
| **Habits** | Inertia | What comfort exists with the status quo? |

**Note:** Adoption happens when Push + Pull > Anxieties + Habits.

---

## Job Map Structure

For digital products, the job typically flows:

| Stage | Description | Pain Point Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| Define | Recognize and frame the problem | Don't know what's wrong |
| Locate | Find options and information | Can't find relevant solutions |
| Prepare | Set up data, integrations, processes | Complex setup |
| Execute | Do the core work | Slow, error-prone |
| Confirm | Check results, share with stakeholders | Hard to verify |
| Evolve | Adjust process, refine setup | Difficult to improve |

## Interview Methodology

### Target Interview Groups

1. **Recent adopters** — Started using your/competing solution in last 3-6 months
2. **Switchers** — Moved from one solution to another
3. **Strugglers** — Still using workarounds (spreadsheets, email, manual processes)

### Timeline-Based Interview Flow

Anchor on a single concrete episode:

1. **Opening:** "Tell me about the last time you [job situation]. When was that?"
2. **Trigger:** "What changed that made this a problem worth addressing?"
3. **Passive looking:** "What did you try at first, casually, if anything?"
4. **Active looking:** "When did you decide you needed a better way? What did you search for?"
5. **Deciding:** "What made you choose X instead of Y or doing nothing?"
6. **Consuming:** "Walk me through how you used it the first few times."

### Probes for Dimensions

- "How did you feel at that moment?" (emotional)
- "Who else cared whether this worked or failed?" (social)
- "What would have happened if you had done nothing?" (stakes)

---

## Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Confusing personas or features with jobs** — Jobs are about progress, not demographics or buttons
2. **Only capturing functional jobs** — Always probe emotional and social stakes
3. **Relying on hypotheticals** — Anchor on specific past episodes, not "would you..."
4. **Over-fitting to early adopters** — Test if jobs appear in mainstream segments too
5. **Treating JTBD as one-time** — Revisit when adoption patterns shift

---

## Validation Checklist

**Job Hypotheses:**
- [ ] Can be explained without mentioning your product
- [ ] Include concrete situation trigger (not just demographic)
- [ ] Can think of 3+ real people/organizations who experience this

**Job Stories:**
- [ ] Supported by real interview quotes
- [ ] Written in customer language (not UI structure)
- [ ] Distinguish main job from related/emotional/social jobs

**Forces Analysis:**
- [ ] Explains both why people switch AND why they stay
- [ ] Identifies specific anxieties preventing adoption
- [ ] Documents habits creating inertia
