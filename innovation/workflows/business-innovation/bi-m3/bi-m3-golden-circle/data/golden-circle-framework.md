# Golden Circle Framework (Sinek)

**Purpose:** Define brand purpose through Simon Sinek's three-layer model: Why (belief), How (approach), What (offering) — communicating from the inside out.

---

## The Three Layers

Simon Sinek's Golden Circle structures brand identity around three concentric circles. Most brands communicate outside-in (What → How → Why). Great brands start from the inside out.

```
        ┌─────────────┐
        │     WHY     │  ← Purpose, cause, belief
        │  ┌───────┐  │
        │  │  HOW  │  │  ← Differentiated approach
        │  │ ┌───┐ │  │
        │  │ │WHA│ │  │  ← Tangible product/service
        │  │ │ T │ │  │
        │  │ └───┘ │  │
        │  └───────┘  │
        └─────────────┘
```

### Why

The purpose, cause, or belief that drives the organization beyond profit.

**Key Questions:**
- What do you believe about the world?
- Why does this company exist beyond making money?
- What would be lost if you stopped existing?

**Criteria:**
- Does NOT mention product, category, or features
- Would survive a complete product pivot
- Connects to a customer emotional job
- Founder can say "I believe this" without hesitation
- Specific enough that competitors wouldn't use the same words

**Format:** "We believe that [statement about the world]."

### How

The unique approach, values, or process that differentiates execution.

**Key Questions:**
- How does your belief change how you build?
- What do you do differently because of what you believe?
- What observable behaviors distinguish you?

**Criteria:**
- Each principle connects explicitly to the Why
- Observable by customers or team members
- Reflects Lean Canvas Unfair Advantage
- Distinct from competitor approaches

**Format:** 2-3 principles: "We [action/approach] because we believe [connection to Why]."

### What

The tangible products or services offered.

**Key Questions:**
- What do you actually make or sell?
- How would a customer describe what you offer?
- What category do you operate in?

**Criteria:**
- Names a recognizable product category
- Uses customer language, not internal terminology
- Plain and specific — no superlatives or purpose language
- Connected to How and Why through the narrative chain

**Format:** 1-2 sentences describing the offering.

---

## The Why-How-What Chain

The complete narrative connects all three layers:

> "We believe [Why]. That is why we [How]. And that is how we deliver [What]."

**Test:** Read the chain aloud. If any link feels forced, the weaker element needs revision.

---

## Validation Tests

### Endurance Test

> "Imagine you pivot to a completely different product in the same domain. Does the Why still hold?"

If no, the Why is too product-specific.

### Authenticity Test

> "Can the founder say this at a dinner table without feeling performative?"

If hesitation, the Why needs revision to reflect genuine conviction.

### Motivation Test

> "Does this make a potential team member or customer want to be part of this?"

If indifference, the Why lacks emotional charge.

## Prerequisites

Before starting Golden Circle:

- Brand Prism (M3 Step 3) complete — Culture facet informs Why
- M1 Conception complete (JTBD emotional jobs, project memo Tenets)
- Working Backwards "why it matters" narrative

## Integration

**Receives from:**
- Brand Prism → Culture facet (strongest Why signals)
- Brand Archetypes → Core motivation (validates Why direction)
- JTBD → Emotional jobs (grounds Why in customer reality)
- Working Backwards → Leader quote, "why it matters"
- Project Memo → Tenets (founder operating beliefs)
- Lean Canvas → Unfair Advantage (informs How)

**Feeds into:**
- Brand Positioning → Why becomes foundation of positioning benefit
- Messaging Architecture → Why = brand promise layer; How = message pillars
- Tone of Voice → Why's emotional register calibrates tone
- M4 Design Brief → Why sets emotional territory for visual identity
- M5 Market Validation → Why as messaging hypothesis to test

---

## Pitfalls

1. **Confusing Why with What** — "We exist to build the best platform" is What, not Why
2. **Generic Purpose** — "We believe in empowering people" is true for every company
3. **Skipping How** — Without How, the Why has no operational impact
4. **Marketing Exercise** — If the Why only appears on About page, it failed
5. **Defining Without Founder** — External parties cannot invent the Why

## Common Why Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Watch Out For |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Injustice correction** | "We believe no one should have to..." | Don't exaggerate the injustice |
| **Democratization** | "We believe [thing] should be accessible to all" | Be specific about what and who |
| **Transformation** | "We believe people can become..." | Connect to real customer desire |
| **Challenge status quo** | "We believe the old way is broken" | Name what's actually broken |
| **Possibility creation** | "We believe the world is better when..." | Make the outcome concrete |

## Signal Sources for Why

| Source | What to Extract | Example Signal |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Brand Prism Culture | Core beliefs and values | "We believe knowledge should flow freely" |
| JTBD Emotional Jobs | What customers need to feel | "Customers need to feel in control" → Why belief about control |
| Working Backwards Quote | Founder's "why it matters" | "No small business should fail for lack of insight" |
| Project Memo Tenets | Founder operating beliefs | "We always show our reasoning" |
| Archetype Motivation | Universal drive behind archetype | Sage motivation: "Understanding creates power" |
